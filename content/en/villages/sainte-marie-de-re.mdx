Sainte-Marie-de-Ré is the largest commune on the island, and the most visited after Saint-Martin. Its size, services and range of beaches make it one of the most practical bases for a family holiday.

## The village

The town centre has genuine life: a good market, supermarket, plenty of restaurants, shops open most of the year. Unlike some of the smaller villages that feel dormant out of season, Sainte-Marie maintains activity year-round thanks to its population base.

## Beaches

Les Grenettes is the star — a long, supervised beach on the pertuis side, popular with families. La Cible is on the Atlantic side and is the island's main kitesurfing and windsurfing hub. Between the two, Sainte-Marie offers more variety than most villages.

## Kitesurfing and windsurfing

La Cible has a consistent south-westerly wind, shallow water and a kitesurfing school. If water sports beyond swimming are part of your plan, this is the main location.

## Practical information

Sainte-Marie has the best supermarket access of any commune. There are regular bus connections along the island's main road. Cycle paths head in both directions.
