Ars-en-Ré is one of France's official most beautiful villages, and the distinction is deserved. Its white church with a black-painted spire — used as a navigation marker by sailors — is the island's most recognisable silhouette. The village has a salt-working history that still shapes its identity.

## The village

The centre of Ars is a tight network of narrow streets, white walls, coloured shutters and flower pots. The market square in front of the church is the social hub. In season there's a lively Friday market. The port is small and authentic.

## Salt

Ars-en-Ré is surrounded by active salt pans. The paludiers (salt workers) still harvest here in summer, and the Ecomuseum of the Salt Marshes gives context to the practice. Fleur de sel from this area is among France's finest.

## The Lilleau des Niges Nature Reserve

Just north of the village, this wetland reserve is one of France's most important migratory bird staging grounds. Spring and autumn migrations are spectacular. The reserve is open year-round; guided tours operate in season.

## Cycling

Ars is a natural midpoint for island cyclists — roughly equidistant from Saint-Martin to the east and Les Portes to the west. The routes through the surrounding marshes are flat and beautiful.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Ars-en-Ré crowded in summer?**
Yes, in July and August the village centre is very busy. Early morning and evening are more peaceful. Off-season it's genuinely tranquil.

**Is the Lilleau des Niges nature reserve accessible without a guide?**
Yes, there are marked trails. A guided tour adds significant value, particularly for bird identification.
