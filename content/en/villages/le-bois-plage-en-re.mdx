Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré sits in the centre of the island with access to both coastlines. It's one of the most popular bases for families and beach-focused visitors, combining excellent Atlantic beaches with good cycling connections.

## The village

The bourg itself is modest — a market square, bakeries, restaurants, bike hire shops and the practical needs of a busy holiday village. It's less atmospheric than La Flotte or Ars but very functional, and its central position makes it easy to reach any other village.

## Beaches

Le Bois-Plage's standout feature is its Atlantic beaches. Le Gros Jonc is a large, open beach with the kind of waves that draw surfers and bodyboarders. Les Gollandières and Le Peu Ragot complete a stretch of coastline that is genuinely impressive. On the pertuis side, calmer options are available.

## Cycling

The island's central cycle path passes through Le Bois-Plage, making it one of the best-connected villages for two-wheeled exploration. Multiple hire shops in the village.

## Surf

Le Gros Jonc is one of the island's main surf spots. Surf schools operate in season, and the consistent Atlantic swell makes it accessible for beginners.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré a good base for the whole island?**
Yes. Its central position means no destination on the island is more than a 45-minute cycle.

**Is the village itself attractive?**
Functional rather than beautiful. If a charming village centre matters, La Flotte or Ars-en-Ré would suit better.
