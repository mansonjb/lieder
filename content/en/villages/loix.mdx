Loix sits on a peninsula jutting into the salt marshes on the north of the island. It's one of the most remote and least commercialised communes — a place that rewards visitors who actively seek quiet rather than stumbling onto it.

## The peninsula

The road to Loix is a single track across the marshes. Egrets, herons and avocets work the mudflats beside it. The village itself is small — a few dozen stone houses, a restaurant or two, the church. Nothing to crowd out the peace.

## Salt marshes and nature

The marshes around Loix are among the island's finest landscapes. Flat, luminous and full of birds, they're ideal for cycling slowly or walking with binoculars. The coastline at Le Boutillon reveals wide mudflats at low tide — excellent for shellfish gathering.

## Shellfish gathering

Le Boutillon is one of the best spots on the island for cockles and periwinkles at low tide. Check tide tables before going and respect the minimum size rules.

## Who comes here

Loix attracts naturalists, cyclists who want a quiet lunch destination, and visitors who have been to the island before and know it well enough to seek out the edges. It's not a first-time destination.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there anywhere to eat in Loix?**
A very limited offer — one or two seasonal restaurants. Check they're open before making a special trip.

**Is Loix worth visiting for a day trip from elsewhere on the island?**
Absolutely. The cycle from La Couarde or Ars through the marshes is beautiful, and Loix itself is a genuinely different register from the more visited villages.
