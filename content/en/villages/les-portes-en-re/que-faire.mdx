Les Portes-en-Ré has very little to do in the organised sense — and that is entirely the point.

## Beaches

Gros Jonc (Les Portes) and La Patache are the village's main assets. Large, wild, Atlantic-facing, backed by dunes. No facilities. Excellent for walking, surfing and kitesurfing. The lack of infrastructure is part of the attraction.

## Cycling to the tip

The cycle to the very end of the island from Les Portes is short. The sense of being at a geographical endpoint — nothing beyond but Atlantic — is satisfying.

## Birdwatching

The northern coast near Les Portes is part of the reserve territory. Seabirds, waders and migratory species in season.

## Nothing else

No museum, no monument, no guided tour. The village exists at its own pace, for its own people. As a visitor, you participate in that pace or you move on.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there anything to keep children busy in Les Portes?**
Beach and cycling. That's it. Sufficient for children who like that; not sufficient for those who need organised activities.

**How far is Les Portes from Saint-Martin?**
About 30 km by road, or 1.5–2 hours cycling. It's a full day trip from the eastern end of the island.
