Les Portes-en-Ré has the island's wildest beaches — Gros Jonc and La Patache — and some of the most dramatic Atlantic coastline.

## Gros Jonc (Les Portes)

Not to be confused with Le Gros Jonc at Le Bois-Plage. This Gros Jonc is larger, wilder and far less visited. Long dune-backed beach, consistent Atlantic swell, no facilities. Kitesurfing and surfing from experienced riders.

## La Patache

Further along the coast, similar character. Very open, windswept and beautiful. Rarely crowded even in August.

## The north coast

The northern shoreline near Les Portes is part-reserve territory. The walking is excellent and the scenery is at the wild end of the island's range.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are the Les Portes beaches supervised?**
No. These are unsupervised wild beaches. Experienced swimmers only.

**How do you get to Gros Jonc and La Patache?**
By bike from the village, via tracks through the dunes. The paths are signed from the village centre.
