Les Portes-en-Ré is the westernmost commune of the island, the quietest, the wildest and the most deliberately uncommercialised. If the Île de Ré has an antidote to its own popularity, it's here.

## Character

The village has no hotel, minimal restaurant infrastructure and a small, tight community. The beaches are superb and virtually undeveloped. The landscape is dune, heather and pine. Getting here requires intention — it's at the end of the road with nothing beyond.

## Beaches

Gros Jonc (Les Portes) and La Patache are long, open Atlantic beaches accessible on foot or by bike from the village. Wide, backed by dunes, with no facilities beyond what you bring yourself. In summer they're used by kitesurfers, surfers and those who specifically want space and emptiness.

## Who comes here

People who have been to the island before and know what they're looking for. Naturalists, cyclists, surfers, and those who find the island's more developed spots overcrowded.

## Getting there

At the far end of the island from the bridge, about 30 km away. By car it's 40 minutes from Rivedoux. By bike from Ars-en-Ré, about an hour through the landscape.
