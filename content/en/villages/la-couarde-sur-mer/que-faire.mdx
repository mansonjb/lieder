La Couarde's central position and access to both coastlines make it a naturally active village. It's a good base for those who want variety.

## Cycling

From La Couarde, the island's main east-west cycle axis runs through the village. Saint-Martin is 20 minutes east, Ars-en-Ré is 25 minutes west through salt marshes. The route south through the marshes toward Loix is particularly beautiful.

## Both coastlines

The Atlantic beach to the north is open and windy, with some wave potential. The pertuis shore to the south is calm and shallow. Having both within 10 minutes on a bike gives unusual flexibility.

## Salt marshes

The southern edge of La Couarde borders active salt pans. Cycling through this landscape — flat, luminous, with birds working the mudflats — is one of the island's essential experiences.

## Market

La Couarde has a market in season, smaller than Saint-Martin or La Flotte but with good local produce.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which beach is better at La Couarde?**
Atlantic side for waves and open-sea drama; pertuis side for calm family swimming. Both valid depending on conditions and preference.

**Is La Couarde a good base for cycling the whole island?**
Excellent. Its central position means shorter cycles in every direction than from the ends of the island.
