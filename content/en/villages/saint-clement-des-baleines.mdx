Saint-Clément-des-Baleines is home to the island's most famous landmark, the Phare des Baleines (Lighthouse of the Whales), which marks the western tip of the Île de Ré. The village itself is small and quiet, popular as the endpoint of cycling tours from the rest of the island.

## The lighthouse

The Phare des Baleines stands 57 metres tall and has guided Atlantic shipping since the 17th century. You can climb it for a panoramic view across the ocean and back along the island's length. In summer, the sunset from the platform is extraordinary.

The adjacent older lighthouse — the Phare des Baleines Ancienne Tour — dates to 1682 and is one of the oldest surviving lighthouses in France.

## La Conche des Baleines

Just below the lighthouse is La Conche des Baleines, a long, wild Atlantic beach backed by pine forest. It's one of the most atmospheric beaches on the island — not particularly good for swimming due to currents and swell, but spectacular to walk.

## The village

Small, quiet, with a few restaurants and rental properties. Not a destination for nightlife or services, but a natural end point for cycling tours from the east.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you climb the Phare des Baleines?**
Yes, though hours vary seasonally. Entry fee applies. The climb involves a significant number of stairs.

**Is there a good sunset from the lighthouse?**
The lighthouse platform faces west over the Atlantic — it's one of the best sunset viewpoints on the island in summer.
