Rivedoux-Plage is the island's entry point, and its activities reflect that position: practical, accessible and a natural starting point for wider exploration.

## Cycling

The bridge cycle path begins here and continues across to mainland La Rochelle — a unique ride with views of the water on both sides. From Rivedoux, the island's main cycle route heads west through every other village. Bike hire is available in the village.

## Beaches

Sablanceaux (Rivedoux Sud) is the main family beach — calm, supervised in season, with good amenities nearby. Good for kayaking and paddleboarding in calm conditions.

## Exploring the bridge

The bridge walkway is accessible from the Rivedoux end. Walking or cycling it gives an unusual perspective on the island's geography.

## Markets and local shopping

A small local market operates in season. The village has a bakery, café and supermarket. It's the most practical stopping point for supplies if arriving by car.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there a surf spot near Rivedoux?**
No — the pertuis-side beaches here are calm. For surfing, head to Le Bois-Plage or La Couarde on the Atlantic side.

**Can you cycle to Saint-Martin from Rivedoux?**
Yes, about 20 minutes on flat cycle paths.
