Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré has some of the island's finest Atlantic beaches — wide, open and backed by dunes, with consistent waves.

## Le Gros Jonc

The main beach. Long, Atlantic-facing, with a consistent beach break. Surf school present in season. Large enough to absorb crowds without feeling packed even in August.

## Les Gollandières

South of Le Gros Jonc, a similar character with arguably fewer people. The pertuis-side access point here gives an alternative on windy days.

## Le Peu Ragot

Wilder and less visited. Backed by dunes, no facilities, Atlantic facing.

## Pertuis access

The eastern (pertuis) coast of Le Bois-Plage offers calmer alternatives when the Atlantic is rough.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are the Le Bois-Plage beaches supervised?**
Le Gros Jonc is supervised in season. The wilder sections are not.

**Are there amenities (toilets, showers) at Le Gros Jonc?**
Yes, basic facilities are present in season.
