Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré's main draw is its beaches, but the village's central position also makes it an ideal base for cycling across the island.

## Atlantic beaches

Le Gros Jonc, Les Gollandières and Le Peu Ragot are the central beaches here — wide, open Atlantic strips with waves suitable for surfing. Surf schools operate in season. These are genuinely impressive beaches by any standard.

## Cycling

From Le Bois-Plage, every point on the island is within 45 minutes on flat cycle paths. Multiple bike hire shops in the village. The routes through the marshes toward Loix or south toward the pertuis coast are particularly scenic.

## Surf

Le Gros Jonc is the main surf spot. Consistent Atlantic swell, beach break, beginner-friendly in smaller conditions. Schools offer lessons from absolute beginners upward.

## Markets and village life

A market operates in the village square in season. The village has good services — bakeries, supermarket, restaurants — making it self-sufficient for a week.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there enough to do in Le Bois-Plage for a week?**
Yes. Beaches, cycling, day trips to the rest of the island, good food. It works particularly well for active visitors.

**Is Le Bois-Plage a good base for children?**
Yes — though note the Atlantic beaches have more wave energy than the pertuis side. The pertuis beach at La Couarde is 10 minutes' cycle away if you need calmer water.
