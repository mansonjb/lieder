La Couarde-sur-Mer occupies the geographic centre of the island, which makes it one of the most practical bases for cycling in all directions. Its access to both coastlines is a genuine advantage, especially for families.

## Two coastlines

La Couarde has an Atlantic beach to the north — open, windy, with waves — and a calmer pertuis shore to the south. Depending on the weather and your mood, you can switch coastlines in under 10 minutes by bike. This flexibility is rare and genuinely useful.

## The village

The bourg is authentic without being touristic. Covered market hall, local shops, decent restaurants. The hollyhocks that line village walls elsewhere on the island are particularly abundant here. Life continues year-round, which the smaller western communes can't always say.

## Cycling connections

From La Couarde, every major route on the island's cycle network is accessible. Saint-Martin is 20 minutes east by flat path. Ars-en-Ré is 25 minutes west through salt marshes. The full island tour passes through.

## Salt marshes

The southern edge of the commune borders the salt marshes. Cycling or walking through this flat, luminous landscape — aigrettes perched on the mudflats, the smell of brine, the geometric patterns of the salt pans — is one of the island's essential experiences.
