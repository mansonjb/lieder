Saint-Martin-de-Ré has the most concentrated list of things to do on the island. History, shops, restaurants, the port and the ramparts all provide meaningful occupation.

## The ramparts

Vauban's 17th-century fortifications encircle the town. The full circuit on foot takes about 45 minutes and gives excellent views over the port, the countryside and on clear days, the mainland. Free and accessible at all times.

## The citadel

The citadel (privately operated, partially accessible) is the centrepiece of the fortifications. Parts of it function as a prison — the island's unusual claim that it has one of France's oldest functioning prisons.

## Musée Ernest Cognacq

The island's main museum, covering its history, salt trade, smuggling past and maritime culture. Well-presented and worth an hour or two.

## The market

Saint-Martin's market is the island's largest, operating daily in season in the main square. For a more local experience, the early morning fish and vegetable market near the port is excellent.

## The port

The quay is the island's social centre in season. Boat trips to Fort Boyard and Île d'Aix depart from here. Café terraces face the water.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does it take to walk the ramparts?**
About 45 minutes at a relaxed pace. Allow more for stops and photos.

**Are there boat trips from Saint-Martin?**
Yes — to Fort Boyard, Île d'Aix and elsewhere. Seasonal operation; book in advance in July-August.
