Saint-Martin has the most varied and highest-density restaurant scene on the island, covering everything from port-side oysters to creative contemporary cuisine.

## The port quay

The main row of restaurants along the harbour is the island's most famous strip. Varied quality — some excellent, some trading on location. The best reserve well in advance.

## The town streets

Away from the port, the town's streets contain smaller, more local restaurants — often better value and less crowded.

## What to eat

Oysters, seafood platters, fish of the day, local lamb, salicorne (samphire). The market provides the best raw ingredients for self-caterers.

## Budget

Saint-Martin tends to be the island's most expensive village for eating out. Set lunch menus (typically 18–25€) remain the best value. Evening à la carte can easily reach 50–70€ per head at the top end.

## Frequently asked questions

**Should I reserve for dinner in Saint-Martin?**
Yes, always in July and August. The best tables fill weeks ahead.

**Is there a good lunch option near the ramparts?**
Several cafés and simpler restaurants near the ramparts and the market square serve reasonable lunches at lower prices than the port restaurants.
