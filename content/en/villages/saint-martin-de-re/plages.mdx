Saint-Martin-de-Ré is surrounded by sea but its beaches are not its strongest suit — most visitors use the town as a base and cycle to beaches elsewhere.

## The town's immediate beaches

The shoreline immediately around Saint-Martin, particularly on the port and northern sides, is more rocky and mudflat than sandy beach. Fine for a stroll along the ramparts or evening walk, not ideal for a family swim.

## Nearby beaches by bike

Cycle 15 minutes east toward La Flotte and Rivedoux for calm pertuis beaches. Cycle 20 minutes west toward Le Bois-Plage for the best Atlantic beaches. The cycle network from Saint-Martin connects directly to both.

## Swimming near town

La Plage de la Cible (pertuis side) near the eastern ramparts has some sandy sections and is calm enough for swimming by the town itself.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why doesn't Saint-Martin have better beaches?**
Its geography — the northern coast here is more rocky than sandy. The town's strengths are elsewhere: fortifications, port, restaurants, history.

**How far is the nearest good beach from Saint-Martin?**
15–20 minutes by bike. It's an easy and pleasant ride on marked paths.
