La Flotte has the best restaurant experience on the island for a quiet port-village lunch. The combination of fresh seafood and the harbour view is hard to beat.

## The port restaurants

Clustered around the square beside the harbour, these restaurants share a formula that works: fish of the day, oysters, mussels, bread and butter, a glass of local white. Simple, fresh, excellent. Reserve for dinner; walk in for lunch (with patience).

## Oysters

La Flotte's commune produces oysters that are among the island's best. The cabanes (oyster shacks) near the port occasionally sell direct. Ask locally.

## Market food

The Wednesday and Saturday morning market is the best place to compose a picnic — cheese, bread, charcuterie, fruit, olives, wine.

## Dinner

La Flotte's evening restaurant offer is smaller than Saint-Martin but the quality is high. Reserve in advance in July and August.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do La Flotte port restaurants get very busy in summer?**
Yes. Walk-in lunches are possible before 12:30 or after 14:00; dinner always better reserved.

**Are there non-seafood options?**
Yes, though seafood dominates. Salads, pasta and grilled meat feature on most menus.
