La Flotte's pleasures are largely those of slow discovery — wandering its streets, attending its market, eating at the port, cycling to the Cistercian ruins in the fields beyond.

## The market

The Wednesday and Saturday morning market at La Flotte is one of the island's best. Local producers, oysters, vegetables, bread. Worth arranging your schedule around at least once.

## The port

Sitting at a port-side café watching the fishing boats is an underrated occupation. The port is busiest in the mornings when boats return.

## Abbaye des Châteliers

A 12th-century Cistercian abbey ruin in the fields between La Flotte and Rivedoux. Accessible on foot (15–20 minutes from the village) or in 5 minutes by bike. In free access, set in open fields, peaceful.

## Fort de la Prée

A 17th-century coastal fort accessible by bike along the southern shore from La Flotte. Well-preserved exterior, worth the detour.

## Cycling

La Flotte is well-connected to the island's cycle network. Routes east toward Rivedoux and west toward Saint-Martin are both flat and easy.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the market at La Flotte better than Saint-Martin's?**
Different in character. La Flotte is smaller but more local in feel; Saint-Martin is bigger and more varied. Both are worth attending.

**Is La Flotte good for children?**
Yes — the beach is calm and supervised, the village is walkable, the market is engaging.
