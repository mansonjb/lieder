Saint-Martin-de-Ré is the island's main town, capital of the commune and the closest thing Ré has to urban life. Its 17th-century Vauban fortifications are a UNESCO World Heritage site, and its port is one of the most photographed in Atlantic France.

## The town

Saint-Martin has more restaurants, bars, shops and things to do per square metre than anywhere else on the island. It's the natural base for anyone who doesn't want to drive or cycle to find a good dinner. The citadel, the ramparts, the port, the market square — all worth time.

## The Vauban fortifications

Designed by Louis XIV's military engineer Vauban, the fortifications that encircle Saint-Martin are an outstanding example of 17th-century military architecture. You can walk the full circuit of the ramparts — it takes around 45 minutes and gives excellent views over the port and countryside.

## The port

Yachts, fishing boats and pleasure craft. Cafés and restaurants line the quay. In season, the port is the social centre of the island's tourist life. Out of season, it's quieter and more authentically French.

## Market and shops

The market at Saint-Martin is the island's largest and most visited. Open mornings most days in season, it covers the town's main square with local produce, flowers, clothes and craft. Year-round shops make it the most practical village for day-to-day needs.

## Where to stay

The most varied hotel offer on the island. Boutique hotels within the fortifications, chambres d'hôtes in the surrounding streets, and rental properties at every price point.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Saint-Martin-de-Ré worth visiting even in winter?**
Yes. The town has permanent residents, open restaurants and shops, and the fortifications are most easily enjoyed without crowds.

**How long does it take to walk the ramparts?**
About 45 minutes at a relaxed pace. Allow more time if you stop to look at the views or visit the citadel.
