Saint-Clément-des-Baleines has two primary draws: the lighthouse and the adjacent wild beach. Most visitors arrive specifically for one of these.

## Phare des Baleines (lighthouse)

The island's most iconic structure. 57 metres tall, climbable (entry fee applies, seasonal hours). The view from the top — ocean to the west, the island's full length to the east — is exceptional. The sunset from the platform is memorable.

## La Conche des Baleines

A long Atlantic beach below the lighthouse, backed by pine forest. More atmospheric than swimmable — currents make it less ideal for bathing, but magnificent for walking.

## Pine forest

The forest between the lighthouse and the village is good for walking. Cooler in summer heat than the exposed beaches.

## Cycling westward

Saint-Clément is the penultimate commune before Les Portes at the island's tip. A day's cycling from Ars to Saint-Clément to Les Portes and back is one of the island's classic routes.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the lighthouse worth the entry fee?**
Yes. The view is genuinely impressive and the experience of being at the island's western tip adds to it.

**Is La Conche des Baleines safe for swimming?**
The currents can be strong. Check conditions before entering the water; it's best treated as a walking beach.
