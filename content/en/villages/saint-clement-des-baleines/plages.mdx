Saint-Clément-des-Baleines has one of the island's most atmospheric stretches of coast — La Conche des Baleines — and a section of the northern Atlantic shore.

## La Conche des Baleines

The beach immediately below the lighthouse. Long, backed by pine trees, wild and beautiful. Strong currents make it less suitable for swimming, but superb for walking and photography.

## Atlantic coast

The western coast north of the village has open beach access. Wild, windswept, no facilities. Good for surfing by experienced riders.

## Setting

The combination of pine forest, lighthouse and open Atlantic beach is the most dramatic coastal setting on the island. Worth visiting for the landscape alone.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is La Conche des Baleines a supervised beach?**
No. Treat with appropriate caution.

**What's the best time to visit for the sunset?**
Any clear summer evening. The lighthouse platform is the best vantage point, but the beach itself gives a direct ocean view of the setting sun.
