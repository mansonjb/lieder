Ars-en-Ré offers a rich mix of cultural, natural and culinary experiences that make it one of the most rewarding villages to spend a few days.

## The Lilleau des Niges Nature Reserve

Just north of the village, this wetland reserve is one of France's most important bird sanctuaries. Spring and autumn migration brings thousands of birds. Guided tours in season. Free entry to the walking trails.

## Salt marshes and the Ecomuseum

The marshes around Ars are still actively worked. The Ecomuseum of the Salt Marshes explains the paludier tradition and the ecology of these ancient salt pans. Tasting included.

## The church and village

The black-spired church is the island's most distinctive landmark. The narrow streets around it reward unhurried exploration. The market (Friday mornings in season) is excellent.

## Cycling the western tip

Ars is the natural staging post for cycling west to Saint-Clément and Les Portes, or east through the marshes to La Couarde. Both routes are beautiful.

## Frequently asked questions

**When is the best time to visit Lilleau des Niges for birds?**
April-May and September-October for migration. Year-round for resident species.

**Is the Ecomuseum suitable for children?**
Yes — it's interactive and engaging, with outdoor sections through the salt pans.
