Ars-en-Ré has limited beach access compared to the island's central and eastern communes — the village's appeal lies more in its village life and nature than in its beaches.

## The pertuis coast

The coast near Ars on the southern side is more marsh and mudflat than sandy beach. Good for shellfish gathering and birdwatching, less suited to swimming.

## Atlantic access

The Atlantic beaches north of Ars — at Les Portes end — are wild and accessible by bike. No facilities, strong wind common, beautiful.

## Nearby beaches by bike

Ars's best beach option is to cycle to the Atlantic coast 10–15 minutes away. The route through the northern marshes is one of the island's most rewarding.

## Frequently asked questions

**Does Ars-en-Ré have a swimming beach?**
Not a classic sandy swimming beach immediately accessible from the village. By bike the options open up considerably.

**Is Ars a good base for beach holidays?**
Better for nature, village life and cycling than beach-focused holidays. La Couarde or Le Bois-Plage are better for beach-first visitors.
