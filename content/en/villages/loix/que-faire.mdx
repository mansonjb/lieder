Loix is not an activities destination in the conventional sense. Its pleasures are quiet ones: cycling through the marshes, shellfish gathering at low tide, watching birds, and absorbing the particular stillness of the island's most remote commune.

## Cycling the marshes

The cycle route from the main island to Loix crosses salt pans and mudflats on a thin road. The landscape is remarkable — flat, wide-skied and full of birds. It's an easy, slow ride best done at a pace that allows attention.

## Shellfish gathering

Le Boutillon, the small beach-cove at Loix, opens wide mudflats at low tide. Cockles and periwinkles are found here. Check tide tables in advance and respect minimum size regulations.

## Birdwatching

The marshes around Loix are excellent for egrets, herons, stilts and other waders. Spring migration brings additional species.

## Simply being

Loix is a place to stop rather than a place to do things. A coffee on a terrace, a slow walk through the village streets, watching the light change over the marshes — that's the activity.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there anything for children to do in Loix?**
Shellfish gathering at low tide is engaging for children. Otherwise it's a quiet environment better suited to adults who appreciate calm.

**Is Loix accessible by bike from Ars-en-Ré?**
Yes — it's about 20 minutes on flat paths through the marshes.
