Loix has a small, quiet beach at Le Boutillon — more of a cove than a beach in the traditional sense, and much better known for shellfish gathering than swimming.

## Le Boutillon

A sheltered cove on the pertuis side, accessible from the village. At low tide the mudflats extend considerably. The setting is peaceful and the views across the marshes are good.

## Shellfish gathering

Le Boutillon is the main draw — cockles and periwinkles at low tide, accessible without specialist equipment. Check tide times before visiting.

## Swimming

Possible, but not Loix's primary beach offer. The water is calm and sheltered, but the setting is more mud and marsh than sand and sunloungers.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Le Boutillon a sandy beach?**
Partly — more mud and sand mixture than pure fine sand. The setting is beautiful but different from the island's main beaches.

**Is it supervised?**
No. This is an unsupervised, very quiet beach.
