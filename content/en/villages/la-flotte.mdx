La Flotte is frequently cited as the most beautiful village on the Île de Ré, and it earns the description. Its natural harbour, cobbled lanes and hollyhock-covered stone houses are photogenic at every turn. It's also a working village, which prevents it from feeling like a theme park.

## The port and town centre

The harbour is the focal point. Fishing and pleasure boats sit alongside each other; nets dry in the sun; the restaurants around the square do simple, excellent seafood. The market — Wednesday and Saturday mornings — is one of the island's best, drawing regulars from across the island.

## The historic centre

The streets away from the port reward slow walking. White walls, coloured shutters, roses and hollyhocks, a 12th-century church, the ruins of the Cistercian Abbaye des Châteliers visible by bicycle through the fields. La Flotte is listed as one of France's most beautiful villages, a designation it justifies.

## The beach

La Flotte's main beach is sheltered, north-facing, and calm. Supervised in season, fine sand, gently shelving. Better for relaxed swimming than for waves.

## Where to eat

The port restaurants are the heart of things. Fresh seafood cooked simply, often with a direct view of the boats. La Flotte rewards a long lunch more than almost anywhere else on the island.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is La Flotte lively in the evenings?**
More so than the quieter western villages, less so than Saint-Martin. Port restaurants are busy until late in season; after mid-September it quiets considerably.

**Is La Flotte accessible without a car?**
Very. The village is compact and walkable, and cycle paths connect to neighbouring communes in both directions.
