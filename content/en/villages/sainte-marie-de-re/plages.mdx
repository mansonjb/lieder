Sainte-Marie-de-Ré has both a calm pertuis beach and an Atlantic beach, giving it more versatility than most island communes.

## Les Grenettes (pertuis side)

The family benchmark. Calm, shallow, long, supervised in season. One of the best-equipped beaches on the island for young children — gradual entry into warm water, with amenities nearby. Accessible by foot from the village centre.

## La Cible (Atlantic side)

The island's main kitesurfing beach. Windy, with choppier water. Not recommended for families looking for a calm swim, but excellent for water sports. School and hire available in season.

## Other beaches

Several smaller access points along the pertuis coast, generally quieter than Les Grenettes.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which beach is better for children?**
Les Grenettes, without question. Shallow, calm and supervised.

**Is La Cible beach suitable for swimming?**
It can be windy and rough. On calmer days it's perfectly fine; on typical Cible days, Les Grenettes is the better swimming choice.
