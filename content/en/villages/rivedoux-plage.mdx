Rivedoux-Plage is the first village you reach after crossing the bridge from La Rochelle. It's the most accessible point on the island and a solid base for first-time visitors who want a practical, well-served location without the tourist intensity of Saint-Martin.

## Location and character

The village sits at the eastern end of the island, separated from the mainland by just over 3 kilometres of bridge. It has a genuine year-round community — bakers, butchers, a pharmacy, a supermarket — which keeps it from feeling like a pure holiday resort. The bridge view from the beach is striking.

## Beaches

Rivedoux has two main beaches: Rivedoux Nord and Rivedoux Sud (Sablanceaux), both on the calmer pertuis side. Sablanceaux is long, sandy and popular with families — the water is shallow and calm, ideal for young children. Neither is a great surf beach, but both are excellent for swimming, kayaking and paddleboarding.

## What to do

The village is a natural start or end point for cycling routes heading west across the island. The bridge walkway and cycle path is a notable feature — cycling across gives a superb view of the coast in both directions. Markets, local shops and a small selection of restaurants cover daily needs comfortably.

## Where to stay

A mix of holiday rentals, a few smaller hotels and chambres d'hôtes. Prices are generally slightly lower than Saint-Martin but higher than the villages at the western end. Good availability compared to the most popular spots.
