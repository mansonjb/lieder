An off-season weekend on the Île de Ré (October-April) offers a completely different experience from summer — local, quiet and beautiful.

## Why it works

The island's character off-season is genuinely distinctive. Wild beaches are empty. The cycle paths are yours alone. Saint-Martin's restaurants serve locals, not tourists. The food is as good.

## What's open

In Saint-Martin: most year-round services. In smaller villages: limited but some. Plan around Saint-Martin as your base.

## The off-season programme

Saturday: drive straight to Loix or Ars for the morning. Marsh walking. Bird reserve. Lunch in Ars. Afternoon: cycle toward the lighthouse. Evening: dinner in Saint-Martin.
Sunday: market (if open), cycle to the Atlantic beaches alone.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the bridge quieter off-season?**
Yes — crossing takes 5 minutes rather than 45 on a summer Saturday.

**Can you swim off-season?**
October: some hardened swimmers yes. November through April: generally no.
