A first visit to the Île de Ré should focus on understanding the island's essential character rather than trying to see everything.

## First-visit priorities

**1. Understand the two coastlines** — spend time on both the pertuis side (calm) and the Atlantic side (open, windy). The contrast is fundamental.

**2. Visit Saint-Martin and La Flotte** — the island's two essential villages. Walk the ramparts. Sit at a La Flotte port café.

**3. Cycle into the salt marshes** — the landscape between La Couarde and Ars is distinctively Île de Ré and nowhere else.

**4. Eat properly** — oysters, fish of the day, fleur de sel on a boiled potato. The food is excellent.

**5. See the lighthouse** — the Phare des Baleines at sunset is the island's iconic view.

## Frequently asked questions

**How many days for a first visit?**
Minimum 3 days to cover the essentials. A week is better.

**Is Île de Ré better than Île d'Oléron for a first Atlantic island visit?**
Île de Ré has more to offer in terms of villages and infrastructure. Oléron is larger and cheaper. Ré is the better choice for a first-time Atlantic island experience.
