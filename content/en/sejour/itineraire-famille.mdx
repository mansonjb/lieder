A family itinerary for the Île de Ré balances beach time, activities and enough variety to keep different ages engaged.

## 5-day family itinerary

**Day 1:** Arrive, settle in. Afternoon at a pertuis beach (Sablanceaux or Les Grenettes). Calm, shallow water for young children.

**Day 2:** La Flotte market morning. Picnic at the beach. Afternoon: bike ride toward Saint-Martin.

**Day 3:** Le Bois-Plage Atlantic beaches. Surf lesson for older children. Evening: village market.

**Day 4:** Ars-en-Ré and the salt marsh ecomuseum. Birdwatching at Lilleau des Niges.

**Day 5:** Phare des Baleines. La Conche des Baleines beach walk. Return home.

## Ages

Works well for children from 4–5 upward. Younger children are happiest at the calm pertuis beaches; older children and teenagers engage more with the Atlantic side and activities.

## Frequently asked questions

**What do teenagers do on Île de Ré?**
Surfing, kitesurfing, kayaking, cycling independence. Teenagers who like outdoor activities do very well here.

**Is there a water park or theme park on the island?**
No. The island's activities are natural. This is its appeal and its limitation for families expecting organised entertainment.
