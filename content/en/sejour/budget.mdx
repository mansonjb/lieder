Understanding the costs of a trip to the Île de Ré helps set realistic expectations and make better planning decisions.

## Accommodation costs (per week, summer)

- Tent pitch at campsite: €200–400/week
- Campsite mobile home (4 people): €800–1,500/week
- Holiday rental, basic (4–6 people): €1,500–2,500/week
- Holiday rental, quality (4–6 people): €2,500–4,500/week
- Hotel room (per night): €100–300/night

## Food costs (per person, per day)

- Budget (market, bakery, self-catering): €20–35/person/day
- Mid-range (1 restaurant lunch or dinner): €40–60/person/day
- Full restaurant dining: €70–100+/person/day

## Activities

- Bike hire: €10–15/day (standard), €25–35 (electric)
- Lighthouse: approximately €5–8 entry
- Museum: approximately €5–8 entry
- Surf lesson: €35–50 for 2 hours

## Total budget estimate

For 4 people, 7 nights, mid-range: accommodation €2,000 + food €1,400 + activities €400 + bridge tolls €40 = approximately €3,800–4,500 total.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Île de Ré more expensive than other French beach destinations?**
Yes — prices are above the French average for the region. Similar to Brittany's more sought-after spots.

**Can you visit on a tight budget?**
Yes — camping, market food, cycling and free beaches keep costs manageable.
