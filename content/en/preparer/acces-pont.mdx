The Viaduc de Ré (often called simply "the bridge") connects the island to the mainland at La Rochelle. It's the only way on and off the island.

## The basics

Length: 2,926 metres. Opened in 1988. 12 booths on the mainland side.

**Toll:** Paid in one direction (mainland to island). Free to return. Prices in 2025: approximately €16 for cars (peak season, April to September), €8 in winter. Buses and motorcycles have different rates.

## Traffic

Saturday changeover day in July-August: expect significant delays at the toll. Arrive before 9am or after 2pm. Wednesday is the least busy weekday.

## Cycle access

A dedicated cycle and pedestrian path runs alongside the bridge — accessible year-round.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you walk across the bridge?**
Yes, using the pedestrian and cycle path.

**Is the bridge ever closed?**
Rarely — only in extreme weather (violent storms). Check local traffic information if severe weather is forecast.
