A few local terms help navigate the island's specific vocabulary.

## Geographic terms

**Pertuis charentais:** The sheltered channel between the island's eastern coast and the mainland. "Pertuis" means strait.

**Viaduc:** The bridge. Locals say "le pont."

**Les Dunes:** The sandy hills backing the Atlantic beaches.

## Produce and gastronomy

**Fleur de sel:** The delicate, hand-harvested crust of sea salt.

**Huîtres plates / creuses:** Flat oysters / cupped oysters (the more common type).

**Mogettes:** White haricot beans typical of the region.

**Pineau des Charentes:** Local fortified wine aperitif.

## Occupations

**Paludier:** Salt worker. Harvests salt from the marsh pans.

**Ostréiculteur:** Oyster farmer.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is French necessary to enjoy the island?**
No — English is widely spoken in tourist areas. But a few words of French are appreciated and practically useful.

**What does "gîte" mean?**
A self-catering holiday property (house or apartment), typically rented directly from the owner.
