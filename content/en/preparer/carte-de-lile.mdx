Understanding the island's geography makes planning much easier.

## The basic layout

A thin sliver of land running southwest to northeast, about 30 km long and 5 km wide at its widest. The bridge connects the eastern end (Rivedoux-Plage) to La Rochelle.

## The two coasts

**Atlantic (northern/western) coast:** Exposed, wild, beaches with waves.

**Pertuis (eastern/southern) coast:** Sheltered, calm, shallow water.

## East to west

From the bridge: Rivedoux → Sainte-Marie → La Flotte → Saint-Martin → Le Bois-Plage → La Couarde → Loix (north peninsula) → Ars-en-Ré → Saint-Clément-des-Baleines → Les Portes-en-Ré.

## The salt marshes

Occupy much of the northern interior, between Ars, Loix and the Atlantic coast.

## Getting a map

The tourism office in Saint-Martin sells good cycle and walking maps. IGN 1:25,000 maps cover the island in detail. The island's cycle path maps are free from hire shops.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does it take to drive across the island?**
Rivedoux to Les Portes: about 40 minutes by road. Much longer if you stop anywhere.

**Can you see the whole island from the lighthouse?**
On a clear day, the lighthouse platform gives a view of the island's full length.
