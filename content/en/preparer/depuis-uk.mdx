Île de Ré is one of the most popular French island destinations for British visitors. It is well connected to the UK by sea, rail and air, and the journey is more straightforward than many people expect.

## Ferry from Portsmouth to La Rochelle (recommended)

Brittany Ferries operates a direct overnight ferry from Portsmouth to La Rochelle, typically running from spring through to autumn. This is the most convenient route for British travellers heading to Île de Ré: you drive onto the ferry in Portsmouth, sleep overnight in a cabin, and wake up in La Rochelle, twenty minutes from the bridge.

Crossing time: approximately 12 hours (overnight). Cabins available at all price points. Catering on board.

Once in La Rochelle, the island is a 20-minute drive. If you are travelling with bikes, the ferry allows them on board: load them in Portsmouth, unload them at La Rochelle port, and cycle directly to the island via the bridge cycle path.

Brittany Ferries also operates **Portsmouth to Saint-Malo** year-round. From Saint-Malo, Île de Ré is a 3-hour drive south. This route has more frequent crossings and can work out cheaper, but adds significant driving time.

## Eurostar and TGV

London St Pancras to La Rochelle by train: approximately 6 to 7 hours total.

1. Eurostar from London St Pancras to Paris Gare du Nord: 2 hours 15 minutes.
2. Short taxi or Metro (line 4 or 13) from Gare du Nord to Paris Montparnasse: 20 to 30 minutes.
3. TGV from Paris Montparnasse to La Rochelle: 2 hours 45 minutes to 3 hours.
4. From La Rochelle station to the island: bus (seasonal Lilas service) or taxi (approximately 25 to 35 euros).

Trains should be booked in advance for summer travel. SNCF Connect and Eurostar sell through tickets.

This route works well if you want to travel without a car. Hire bikes are available on the island in every village.

## Flights

Several UK airports operate direct flights to La Rochelle in summer: Bristol, Southampton and Edinburgh among others. Ryanair and others run routes that vary by season. Flight time is approximately 1 hour 20 minutes from southern England.

From La Rochelle airport to the island: taxi (approximately 35 to 45 euros) or the Lilas bus. The airport is west of La Rochelle, slightly closer to the bridge than the train station.

Flying to **Bordeaux** is another option, with more year-round routes from the UK. Bordeaux to Île de Ré is approximately 2 hours by car.

## Driving via the Channel Tunnel

Le Shuttle from Folkestone to Calais: 35 minutes. From Calais, drive south on the A10/A83 motorway to the Île de Ré bridge: approximately 5.5 to 6 hours (excluding stops and traffic). Total door-to-door from London: around 8 to 9 hours.

This route makes sense if you want maximum flexibility on the island and are already travelling with bikes, surfboards or other equipment.

## What works best

**For most British families**: Portsmouth to La Rochelle ferry. Overnight, comfortable, you arrive rested with your car and everything in it. The Saturday night sailing means you wake up in La Rochelle on Sunday morning, ready to drive straight to the island.

**For couples or solo travellers without a car**: Eurostar and TGV. Bike hire is easy and affordable on the island.

**For a short break from the south of England**: Ryanair or similar to La Rochelle. Fast but requires hiring a car or relying on taxis.

## Practical notes

- The bridge toll applies to all vehicles entering the island. In peak season (July and August), expect queues on Saturday afternoons and Sunday evenings. The Sunday evening return rush is significant; leaving the island by midday avoids the worst of it.
- British bank cards and contactless payments are widely accepted on the island.
- English is spoken at most hotels and rental agencies, particularly in the main villages.
- The island has no large supermarket. The main Champion/Intermarche in Sainte-Marie-de-Ré is the largest grocery option. Stocking up in La Rochelle on arrival is worth considering.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there a direct ferry from the UK to Île de Ré?**
Not to the island itself, but Brittany Ferries runs Portsmouth to La Rochelle, which is 20 minutes from the bridge. This is effectively a direct connection.

**Can you take bikes on the Portsmouth to La Rochelle ferry?**
Yes. Brittany Ferries accepts bikes. You load them at Portsmouth and collect them at La Rochelle. Combined with the cycle path over the bridge, you can cycle from the ferry terminal to the island.

**How far is Île de Ré from London?**
By ferry and car, approximately 12 hours sailing plus around 30 minutes from La Rochelle. By Eurostar and TGV, approximately 6 to 7 hours total. By air, around 3 hours including the flight and transfer.

**Do British visitors need a visa for France?**
No. UK citizens can travel to France without a visa for stays of up to 90 days in any 180-day period. A valid passport is required.

**When do British families typically visit?**
Late July and August, aligned with UK school summer holidays. September is increasingly popular: quieter beaches, lower prices and warm sea temperatures (21 to 23 degrees in early September). The island is less visited out of season, though many restaurants and rental properties close from October.
