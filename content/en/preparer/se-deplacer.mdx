Getting around the Île de Ré once you're there is simple — the island is small and the cycle path network covers everything.

## By bike (recommended)

Over 100 km of dedicated cycle paths. Flat terrain. Every village connected. The correct way to experience the island. Hire from shops in every village.

## By car

Fine for reaching your accommodation and making supply runs. Less useful day-to-day — parking is limited at beaches and in village centres in summer. Traffic on the main road in peak season.

## By bus

The Lilas bus serves the main villages with a seasonal schedule. Useful for specific journeys but not flexible enough for general exploring.

## On foot

Good within villages and for coastal path walks. Impractical for moving between villages.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do you need a car on Île de Ré?**
For most visitors: no. A car is useful if you have mobility issues, lots of luggage or specific needs. Otherwise the bike covers everything better.

**Can you rent electric bikes?**
Yes, at most hire shops. Book in advance for July-August.
