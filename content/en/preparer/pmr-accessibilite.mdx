The Île de Ré is making progress on accessibility for visitors with reduced mobility, though it remains an island with significant terrain challenges.

## Cycle paths

The island's cycle paths are generally flat and smooth — an electric mobility scooter or adapted bike can use much of the network.

## Beaches

Several beaches have tiralo amphibious wheelchairs for entry into the water (Sablanceaux, Les Grenettes, Le Bois-Plage). Book in advance through the relevant commune.

## Accommodation

Some properties have ground-floor accessible bedrooms. Filter specifically when searching.

## Village access

Saint-Martin-de-Ré's historic centre has cobbled streets that are challenging for wheelchair users. The port area is more accessible.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the island accessible for wheelchair users?**
Partially. The cycle paths and flat terrain help, but beach and village centre access has limitations.

**Where to get information on accessibility?**
The Île de Ré tourism office in Saint-Martin centralises current accessibility information.
