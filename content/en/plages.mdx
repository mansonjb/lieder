The Île de Ré has two very different coastlines, and understanding which suits you will save time.

## Atlantic coast (western shore)

The western side faces the open Atlantic. Beaches here are wide, long, wild and often windy. The swell can make them less suitable for young children but ideal for surfing, kitesurfing and anyone who wants dramatic scenery. No lifeguards outside peak season. No cafés. Bring everything you need.

The major Atlantic beaches: Les Gollandières, Le Gros Jonc (Le Bois-Plage), La Couarde Atlantic, Trousse-Chemise, Gros Jonc (Les Portes), La Patache, La Conche des Baleines.

## Pertuis coast (eastern shore)

The eastern side faces the Pertuis Charentais, the sheltered channel between the island and the mainland. Water here is calmer, shallower and warmer — ideal for families with young children. Rivedoux, Sablanceaux, Les Grenettes (Sainte-Marie), La Cible.

## Supervised beaches

Lifeguard cover operates on the most popular beaches in July and August. Flags: green = safe, yellow = caution, red = no swimming. Outside supervised zones and seasons, all judgement is your own.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are dogs allowed on the beaches?**
Not from May to September on most beaches. From October to April, dogs on leads are generally permitted.

**Which beach is best for young children?**
Sablanceaux at Rivedoux, Les Grenettes at Sainte-Marie, and the pertuis-side beaches at La Couarde are all calm and shallow.
