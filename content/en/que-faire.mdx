The Île de Ré has more to do than most people expect from a small French island. The beaches are the main draw, but cycling, wildlife, history and gastronomy all offer strong reasons to explore beyond the sand.

## Cycling

The island's cycle path network covers over 100 kilometres and connects every village. Hiring bikes for a day or week is the standard way to get around, and routes exist for every level — flat coastal runs, loops through salt marshes, and the full island circuit for those with the legs.

## Beaches and water sports

Surfing, kitesurfing and windsurfing on the Atlantic side. Kayaking, paddleboarding and family swimming on the calmer eastern shore. La Cible beach at Sainte-Marie is the main kitesurfing hub.

## Nature and wildlife

The Lilleau des Niges nature reserve in the north of the island is one of France's most important migratory bird staging posts. Birdwatching is exceptional here in spring and autumn. The salt marshes of Loix and Ars support a different community of waders and specialist plants.

## History and heritage

Saint-Martin's Vauban fortifications are a UNESCO World Heritage site. The Fort de la Prée on the eastern coast dates to the same era. The Musée Ernest Cognacq in Saint-Martin covers the island's history, salt trade and maritime past.

## Markets

Every village has a market. Saint-Martin's is the biggest. La Flotte's Wednesday and Saturday markets are excellent. Ars-en-Ré has a lively Friday market in season.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there enough to do for a week?**
Easily. A week allows you to explore most villages, try several beaches, do a few cycling routes, visit the lighthouse and nature reserve, and eat well every day.

**What is there to do on a rainy day?**
The Ernest Cognacq museum, the Phare des Baleines (lighthouse), the ecomuseum at the salt marshes, and shopping in Saint-Martin are the best indoor or weather-resilient options.
