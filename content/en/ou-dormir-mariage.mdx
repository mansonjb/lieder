Île de Ré is a genuinely exceptional wedding destination — the combination of Atlantic light, whitewashed stone architecture, sea views and first-rate restaurants creates the conditions for memorable celebrations.

## Why Île de Ré for a wedding

The island's aesthetic is consistent: pale stone, blue shutters, climbing roses, salt-washed air. Photographs look as if they've been professionally colour-graded even before editing. Venues range from formal château-style properties to rustic waterfront settings.

## Practical considerations

Peak wedding season mirrors peak tourist season: June, July and August. Book venues and accommodation a year or more in advance. The bridge is a potential bottleneck for large guest convoys — advise guests to arrive staggered or well in advance.

## Accommodation for guests

Saint-Martin-de-Ré has the most hotel rooms for guests who don't want to rent a house. La Flotte and Ars-en-Ré also have options. Many couples block-book a gîte or rental property for the bridal party.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can non-residents get married on Île de Ré?**
Yes, though civil marriage requires specific administrative steps with the local commune. Many couples have a civil ceremony elsewhere and hold the celebration on the island.

**What is the best month for a wedding on Île de Ré?**
June or September: warm, beautiful light, less peak-season chaos than July-August.
