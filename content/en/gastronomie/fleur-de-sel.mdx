Fleur de sel from the Île de Ré is one of France's great artisan food products — harvested by hand in a specific way from the island's salt marshes.

## What it is

Fleur de sel is the delicate crust that forms on the surface of the salt pans in hot, dry, windy conditions. It's harvested by hand with a flat wooden rake (the lousse) and is nothing like industrial salt.

## Flavour and use

Lighter, less sharp and more complex than regular salt. Used as a finishing salt — sprinkled on food just before serving rather than cooked in. Classic uses: crushed on grilled fish, on a good steak, with good butter and bread, on chocolate desserts.

## Île de Ré vs Guérande

Both are famous French fleur de sel. Subtle flavour differences depending on the specific mineral content of the clay pans. Both are excellent.

## Where to buy

Markets, the Ecomuseum gift shop, specialist food shops throughout the island and in La Rochelle. Available in paper bags or ceramic pots.

## Frequently asked questions

**Why is fleur de sel more expensive than regular salt?**
It's harvested by hand in small quantities under specific weather conditions. The paludier's skill and the weather's cooperation both limit supply.

**Does fleur de sel taste very different from regular sea salt?**
Yes, noticeably. Lighter on the palate, less abrasively salty, more complex.
