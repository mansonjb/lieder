The Île de Ré potato is one of France's rare AOP (Appellation d'Origine Protégée) vegetables — a certification that ties the flavour of these small, firm potatoes to the specific combination of island soil and sea air.

## What makes them special

The island's sandy, salt-influenced soil produces potatoes with a distinctive flavour — slightly saline, nutty, with a firm texture that holds up well to boiling. The most famous variety, La Bonnotte, is grown almost exclusively on Noirmoutier, but Ré potatoes are their equal in their own right.

## Season

The Île de Ré potato harvest begins in May — among the earliest in France. The arrival of the first potatoes at the market is a genuine seasonal event.

## How to cook them

Boiled with their skins on, served with good butter and salt (naturally). They need nothing more. Pan-fried in duck fat. Served cold in a vinaigrette salad.

## Where to buy

Markets throughout the island from May onward. Some direct producer sales.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you buy Île de Ré AOP potatoes outside the island?**
In specialist food shops and some supermarkets on the mainland, in limited quantities.

**Are they worth the price premium?**
Yes — the flavour genuinely is different. Worth buying at the market when in season.
