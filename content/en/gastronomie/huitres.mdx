Île de Ré oysters are among the finest in France — a combination of Atlantic water quality, the pertuis charentais environment and traditional cultivation methods.

## The oysters

Both flat oysters (huîtres plates, Ostrea edulis) and cupped oysters (huîtres creuses, Crassostrea gigas) are produced in the island's beds. Cupped oysters are the more common type available; flat oysters are rarer and more expensive.

## The flavour

Ré oysters have a clean, briny flavour with mineral character. The pertuis charentais gives them a specific terroir — different from Brittany oysters, different from Arcachon.

## How to eat them

Open them yourself if you have a proper oyster knife and cloth. Eat with bread and unsalted butter. A squeeze of lemon or a splash of vinaigrette with shallots is optional — many prefer them plain.

## Buying

Markets, cabanes ostréicoles (direct from producer) and any seafood restaurant on the island.

## Frequently asked questions

**What size of Île de Ré oyster should I order?**
French oysters are numbered 0–5, with 0 being largest. A size 2 or 3 is the standard all-purpose size for most people.

**Are Île de Ré oysters better than Brittany oysters?**
Different. Ré oysters tend to be cleaner and more mineral; some Breton oysters are nuttier and fatter. Both are excellent.
