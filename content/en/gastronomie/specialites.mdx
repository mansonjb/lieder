The Île de Ré has a distinctive culinary identity, built around a combination of its marine environment and the Atlantic agricultural tradition.

## The core specialities

**Oysters** — the island's primary gastronomic identity. See the oyster page for detail.

**Fleur de sel** — the hand-harvested salt that is one of France's finest condiments.

**AOP potatoes** — the Île de Ré potato, harvested in May-June, with a specific flavour tied to the island's soil.

**Pineau des Charentes** — the local fortified wine aperitif made from unfermented grape juice and Cognac.

**Mogettes** — white haricot beans typical of the Vendée and Charente-Maritime coastal zone. Often served with smoked ham.

**Local lamb** — pre-salé lamb (raised on salt marsh grass) appears on some menus; richer in flavour than standard lamb.

**Fresh fish** — sea bass, sole, dorade and others from the daily catch.

## Frequently asked questions

**What should I absolutely eat on Île de Ré?**
Oysters at a market or cabane, the first AOP potatoes of the season, fleur de sel with good butter and bread.

**Is there a local cheese?**
Not a distinctive local cheese. Charente-Maritime goat cheeses from the mainland appear on cheese boards.
