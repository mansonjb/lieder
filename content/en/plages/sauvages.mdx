The wild beaches of the Île de Ré are among its most valuable assets. No car parks, no sun lounger hire, no beach restaurants — just sea, sand, wind and space.

## The Atlantic coast

The western shore, from La Couarde to Les Portes-en-Ré, is the most exposed and least developed. Le Goisil, Les Gollandières on the wild side, Trousse-Chemise, Gros Jonc at Les Portes and the coastal strips around Les Portes are all accessible by bike, with few or no services.

## Common characteristics

Low dunes or heath behind the beach, persistent wind from the Atlantic, regular swell, no supervision. Access is often via sandy tracks that discourage cars.

## How to get there

By bike from the villages — the island is small enough that nothing is very far. Bring water, food and sun cream. Nothing is available on site.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are the wild beaches genuinely uncrowded?**
Less busy than the developed beaches, though some (Trousse-Chemise, Gros Jonc Le Bois-Plage) fill up in August. The Les Portes beaches remain the most preserved.

**Is naturism practised on wild beaches?**
Some isolated sections informally accommodate naturists, with no official designation.
