The tides of the Île de Ré are part of daily life on the island. Understanding them transforms your visit, especially for shore fishing, swimming and exploring the mudflats.

## The tidal range

The island experiences a significant tidal range. In spring tides (coefficients near 120), the difference between high and low water can reach 5–6 metres. Some pertuis coast beaches almost disappear at high tide and reveal wide mudflats at low.

## Coefficients

Tidal coefficients run from 20 (neap tides, minimal range) to 120 (spring tides, maximum range). Spring tides correspond to new and full moon periods. Coefficients are published annually by the SHOM (French Hydrographic and Oceanographic Service).

## Apps and resources

The **Marée.info** app is the French reference: reliable, free, giving hourly times and heights for every coastal commune. The SHOM also publishes official data.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do tidal times change every day?**
Yes. High tide shifts by approximately 50 minutes each day. Monday's high tide is not at the same time as Tuesday's.

**How can I tell if a beach will be accessible when I arrive?**
Check Marée.info before setting out. Aim to arrive mid-incoming or early outgoing tide for the best swimming conditions.
