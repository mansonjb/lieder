Trousse-Chemise is one of the Île de Ré's most photographed beach names and a genuinely distinctive Atlantic spot, backed by a pine forest that shelters the dunes.

## Character

The beach sits at the western end of the La Couarde commune, accessed through pine trees that open suddenly onto a wide Atlantic strip. The combination of forest shade and open sea, pine-scented air and salt wind, is one of the island's most memorable sensory experiences.

## Conditions

Atlantic exposure means waves and wind. Swimming is possible in calm conditions but this is not a designated family swimming beach. Beautiful for walking along the waterline.

## Photography

The light at Trousse-Chemise in the late afternoon — pine silhouettes backlit against a golden Atlantic — is exceptional. One of the island's great photography locations.

## Access

By bike from La Couarde, through the pine forest track. No facilities on the beach itself — the pines provide shade.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Trousse-Chemise a good swimming beach?**
In calm conditions, yes. In typical Atlantic wind, better for walking than swimming.

**How do you get there?**
Bike is ideal. The forest track from La Couarde takes about 10 minutes.
