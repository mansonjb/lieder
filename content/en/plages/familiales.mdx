The Île de Ré has excellent beaches for families with young children, concentrated on the calmer pertuis side of the island.

## The best family beaches

**Sablanceaux (Rivedoux-Plage)** — first beach after the bridge, calm, supervised, very shallow. A natural first stop.

**Les Grenettes (Sainte-Marie-de-Ré)** — the island's most family-oriented beach, with gradual entry, calm water and good facilities.

**La Couarde pertuis side** — sheltered, good for paddling and kayaking.

**La Flotte beach** — calm, accessible from the village on foot, supervised in season.

## What makes a family beach

Shallow, calm water that warms up fast. Gradual entry. Supervision in season. Facilities (toilets, showers, snacks) nearby. Easy parking or access on foot.

## Atlantic beaches for families

The Atlantic side is more demanding. Le Gros Jonc at Le Bois-Plage is popular with families but has real wave energy — fine for older children, more caution needed for toddlers.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which beach is the calmest for very young children?**
Sablanceaux and Les Grenettes, both on the pertuis side, are the calmest and shallowest.

**Are there water sports for children on the island?**
Yes — kayaks and paddleboards for rent at several beaches, surf lessons from around age 8 upward.
