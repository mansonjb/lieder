Taking a dog to the beach on the Île de Ré requires understanding the seasonal regulations, which vary by commune.

## The general rule

From May to September, dogs are banned from most beaches on the island. This is strictly enforced in July-August on supervised beaches.

## Off-season

From October to April, dogs are generally permitted on most beaches on a lead. Responsible behaviour (cleaning up, controlling the dog) is standard.

## Alternative zones

Even in season, coastal paths, back-dune areas and some quieter stretches are less controlled. The wild western coast is less rigorously policed.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do accommodation properties accept pets?**
Some do. Search specifically using "pets welcome" filters on booking platforms and confirm before booking.

**Are cycling routes accessible with dogs?**
Yes, cycle paths accept dogs on leads. Take care in summer heat.
