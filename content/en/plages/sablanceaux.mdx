Sablanceaux is the first major beach after the bridge — immediately accessible and one of the island's most reliable family options.

## Location

On the pertuis side of Rivedoux-Plage, southeast-facing, with a view of the mainland and the bridge. The setting is distinctive — unusual on the island for having the bridge as a visual element.

## Swimming conditions

Calm, protected, very shallow on the incoming tide. Good for children who are not strong swimmers. Supervised in season.

## Facilities

Showers, toilets and snack options in season. Kayak hire available at the beach.

## Access

From the village of Rivedoux, a short walk or bike ride. Easy parking nearby compared to the more popular beaches further along the island.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Sablanceaux the best beach for young children?**
One of the best, along with Les Grenettes at Sainte-Marie. Very calm and shallow.

**Does the bridge ruin the view?**
That's subjective. Some find it an interesting architectural element; others prefer unobstructed natural horizons. On the positive side, it's a unique view for an island beach.
