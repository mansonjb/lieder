The beach at the Phare des Baleines occupies a unique position at the western tip of the island, directly below France's tallest lighthouse on the Île de Ré.

## The combination

Visiting the beach and climbing the lighthouse together is the natural programme. The walk from the lighthouse car park through the pine trees to the beach takes about 5 minutes.

## Swimming conditions

The bay here provides some shelter from the most direct Atlantic exposure, but currents can be present. Better for walking and appreciating the setting than for serious swimming. Not supervised.

## Pine forest

The forest between the road and the beach is cool, shaded and worth spending time in — especially on hot summer days.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the Phare des Baleines beach the same as La Conche des Baleines?**
They overlap — La Conche des Baleines refers to the same bay and beach below the lighthouse.

**Is there parking at the lighthouse?**
Yes, a car park serves both the lighthouse and the beach. Gets busy in summer; arrive early or visit outside peak hours.
