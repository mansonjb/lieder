A quieter beach in an area known more for its nature reserve than its bathing. Good for walks and birdwatching.

## Location and character

A beach on the northern coast of the island, in the Ars-en-Ré area. The northern coast here has a different character from the Atlantic or pertuis sides — more exposed to north winds, with views across to the mainland coast further north.

## Access

By bike from Ars-en-Ré, following coastal tracks northward.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Le Lizay a supervised beach?**
No — it's a quiet, natural beach without lifeguard cover.

**What's the scenery like?**
Open views northward, salt marsh nearby, birds working the shoreline. More dramatic than beautiful.
