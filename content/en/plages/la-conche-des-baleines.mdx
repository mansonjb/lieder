La Conche des Baleines is the beach directly below the Phare des Baleines lighthouse at Saint-Clément-des-Baleines — atmospheric, wild and backed by pine forest.

## Character

The combination of lighthouse, pine forest and wide Atlantic beach makes this one of the most scenically striking spots on the island. The beach itself is long, sheltered by the curve of the bay from the most direct ocean swell, but still exposed.

## Swimming

Currents can be unpredictable. The beach is better treated as a walk than a swim — take local advice on conditions before entering. Not supervised.

## The lighthouse

The Phare des Baleines is immediately above the beach. Visiting both together is the natural combination — climb the lighthouse for the view, then descend to the beach.

## Pine forest

The forest between the lighthouse and the beach provides shade and a beautiful transition from formal lighthouse to wild coastline.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is La Conche des Baleines safe for swimming?**
With caution. Currents can be strong. Check conditions; it's best treated as a walking beach.

**Can you walk from the lighthouse to the beach?**
Yes, it's a short walk through the pines. The car park serves both.
