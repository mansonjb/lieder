The Île de Ré is not a competition surf destination, but it offers consistent, accessible waves — ideal for beginners and intermediate surfers.

## The spots

The Atlantic coast (western facade) generates the best waves. Les Gollandières and Le Gros Jonc at Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré, La Couarde's Atlantic beach, and sections of the coast near Les Portes are the most used spots.

## Conditions

Atlantic swell is moderate but regular in autumn and spring. Summer is often calmer. Best conditions come with Atlantic low-pressure systems in autumn and spring. Wave height: typically 0.5–1.5m, occasionally 2m in strong swells.

## Schools and hire

Several surf schools operate on the island, particularly at Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré. Beginners' lessons typically run 35–50€ for a 2-hour session.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Île de Ré a good place to learn to surf?**
Yes. Waves are generally gentle and consistent — well-suited to beginners. Less appropriate for experienced surfers seeking power waves.

**What's the best season for surfing?**
September-October and March-April offer the best conditions. Summer is often too calm.
