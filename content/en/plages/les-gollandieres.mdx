Les Gollandières is a substantial Atlantic beach between Le Bois-Plage and La Couarde — wide, exposed and one of the island's less crowded major beaches.

## Character

Long Atlantic beach with dune backing, facing west. Consistent swell and wind. Good for surfing and bodysurfing. The pertuis-side access here gives a calmer alternative on the other side of the dune ridge.

## Conditions

Open Atlantic — winds and waves more persistent than the calmer eastern coast. An experienced swimming beach rather than a family introduction to the sea.

## Access

From Le Bois-Plage or La Couarde by bike. Some car access but parking is limited. No facilities on the beach itself.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Les Gollandières less crowded than Le Gros Jonc?**
Typically yes — it's slightly less well-known and accessed by slightly fewer people.

**Is it suitable for beginner surfers?**
It can be, depending on conditions. Smaller days are manageable for beginners; bigger swell is for experienced riders.
