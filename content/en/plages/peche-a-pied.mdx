Shore fishing (pêche à pied) is a tradition on the Île de Ré, practised at low tide on the northern and eastern coasts. It's a free, accessible family activity with a direct gustatory reward.

## When to go

At low tide, ideally during spring tides (coefficients above 90). Tide tables are available from the SHOM website or the Marée.info app. The 2 hours before and after low tide are the most productive.

## Best spots

Loix (Le Boutillon), the northern coast (Lilleau des Niges area at low tide), the coast around Rivedoux. The eastern (pertuis) coast is more productive than the Atlantic side.

## What you'll find

Cockles, clams, grey shrimp, periwinkles, crabs, mussels. Yield varies by zone and season.

## Rules

Minimum sizes apply (clams: 4cm, cockles: 2cm). Daily quotas for personal consumption (5kg per person maximum). Commercial fishing is prohibited.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do you need a licence for shore fishing on Île de Ré?**
No, for recreational gathering. Respect size limits and quotas.

**What footwear?**
Wellington boots or closed-toe sandals. Some zones are muddy.
