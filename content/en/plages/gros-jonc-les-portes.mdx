Gros Jonc at Les Portes-en-Ré is the western end of the island's great Atlantic beach arc — wide, wild and almost entirely undeveloped.

## Character

Not to be confused with Le Gros Jonc at Le Bois-Plage (which is further east and more visited). This Gros Jonc at Les Portes is larger, quieter and more remote. Backed by dunes, facing open Atlantic, accessible by bike track from the village.

## Conditions

Consistent swell, strong wind on many days. Kitesurfing and surfing territory. Not ideal for families with very small children in typical Atlantic conditions.

## Emptiness

Even in August this beach is noticeably less crowded than the equivalent beaches further east. The distance from the bridge is a natural filter.

## Access

From Les Portes village, 10–15 minutes by bike on dune tracks. No car access. No facilities. Bring water and food.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Gros Jonc at Les Portes supervised?**
No. One of the island's unsupervised wild beaches.

**Is it worth cycling this far from Saint-Martin for this beach?**
For wild beach enthusiasts, absolutely. It's a full day's cycling from Saint-Martin but a rewarding destination.
