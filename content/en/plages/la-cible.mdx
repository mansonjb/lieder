La Cible is the Île de Ré's primary kitesurfing and windsurfing beach, located on the Atlantic side of Sainte-Marie-de-Ré. It's a specialist beach — good for what it does, but less suitable for relaxed family swimming.

## Character

Facing southwest, consistently windy, with shallow water extending over a wide area. These conditions make it almost perfect for kitesurfing and windsurfing — the two sports that define the beach's identity.

## Kitesurfing

A kite school operates in season. The shallow water gives riders confidence to fall without injury, and the consistent wind allows genuine progression. La Cible has an established kitesurfing community.

## Swimming

The wind and the shallow water make for choppier conditions than the pertuis beaches. On typical La Cible days it's perfectly swimmable but not the most comfortable. Families seeking a relaxed swim should head to Les Grenettes (a short cycle away).

## Access

From Sainte-Marie-de-Ré village, cycling south to the Atlantic side. Well-signed and accessible. Parking in season.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is La Cible suitable for complete beginners to kitesurfing?**
Yes — the school at La Cible specifically caters for beginners and the conditions support learning.

**What's the wind like at La Cible?**
South-westerly dominants, typically 15–25 knots in good conditions. Consistent rather than extreme.
