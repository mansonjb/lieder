The Île de Ré is progressively improving beach accessibility for people with reduced mobility. Several communes have put specific measures in place.

## Available facilities

**Tiralo** — amphibious wheelchair allowing assisted entry into the water. Available in season at certain beaches (Sablanceaux at Rivedoux, Les Grenettes at Sainte-Marie, Le Bois-Plage beaches). Advance booking required through the commune or tourism office.

**Accessible paths** — hardened access ways, boardwalks over sand, disabled parking. Availability varies by commune.

## How to find out

Before your visit, contact the Île de Ré Tourism Office which centralises information on in-season accessibility facilities. Communes are required to publish their accessibility level.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are all beaches on Île de Ré accessible by wheelchair?**
No. Beaches with fine sand and no hardened access are difficult to reach. Check specifically which equipped beaches exist before travelling.

**Are tiralo chairs available all season?**
Primarily July-August. Book in advance as demand is high.
