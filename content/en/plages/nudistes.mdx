The Île de Ré has no officially designated naturist beach, but certain isolated sections of the wild coast have been informally used for naturism for many years.

## The practice on the island

Zones where naturism is practised informally are on the Atlantic coast, in the most isolated sections. They are not officially signposted.

## Discretion and tolerance

Local tolerance is real but context-dependent. Away from peak season, the practice is broadly accepted in remote zones. In midsummer, the presence of families and mainstream bathers calls for more discretion.

## Alternatives

For organised naturism with dedicated facilities, the naturist beaches of Île d'Oléron or naturist campsites in Charente-Maritime offer a more structured context.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there an official naturist beach on Île de Ré?**
No, there is no officially designated naturist beach. The practice exists informally in isolated areas of the wild coast.

**Is naturism tolerated everywhere on the island?**
No. Outside isolated Atlantic coast areas, tolerance is limited and discretion required.
