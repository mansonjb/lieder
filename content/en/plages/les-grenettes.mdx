Les Grenettes at Sainte-Marie-de-Ré is the island's benchmark family beach — calm, supervised, well-served and generally excellent.

## Why it works so well

The pertuis side of Sainte-Marie provides naturally sheltered water. The beach has a gradual entry, fine sand and consistent summer supervision. Facilities (toilets, showers, restaurants nearby) are available. It's the full package for families.

## Supervision

Lifeguard cover operates in July and August. Green, yellow and red flags indicate swimming conditions.

## Activities

Kayak hire, paddleboards and small sailing dinghies available in season. The shallow water is well-suited to paddling and low-impact water play.

## Getting there

From the village centre of Sainte-Marie, walkable in 10–15 minutes. Cycle path access. Parking available in season with some distance involved.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Les Grenettes crowded in summer?**
Yes in July-August — it's one of the island's most popular beaches. Early morning and late afternoon are the best times for space.

**Is the water warm at Les Grenettes?**
Warmer than the Atlantic side — the pertuis is a sheltered channel that heats faster. Typically 19–21°C in July-August.
