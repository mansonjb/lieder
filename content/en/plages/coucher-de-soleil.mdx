The sunsets on the Île de Ré are one of its great pleasures. The island's west-facing Atlantic coast and long summer evenings (sun setting after 9:30pm at midsummer) create conditions for spectacular light.

## Best spots

**Phare des Baleines** is the iconic viewpoint. The observation platform at 57 metres gives 360° views across a reddening Atlantic. In summer, the sun sets after 9:30pm, sometimes past 10pm.

**Les Portes-en-Ré west coast** — Gros Jonc and La Patache, vast, empty beaches facing a horizon with nothing between you and America.

**Trousse-Chemise** — the combination of pine silhouettes, setting sun and sand is exceptional.

**Le Goisil and the wild coast** between Loix and Ars.

## Best season

June and July have the latest and most golden sunsets. September offers warmer light and often dramatic cloudy skies.

## Frequently asked questions

**What time does the sun set on Île de Ré in summer?**
Approximately 9:45pm in late June, 9:30pm in late July, 9:00pm in mid-August. Local summer time.

**Can you see the green flash from Île de Ré?**
Theoretically yes, in perfect conditions — clear marine horizon, no haze. Rare but reported.
