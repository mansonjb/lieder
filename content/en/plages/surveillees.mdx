Beach supervision on the Île de Ré is concentrated on the peak summer period and the most frequented beaches.

## When supervision operates

Lifeguard posts (MNS) are deployed in July and August on the busiest beaches. Some posts open in late June depending on the commune.

## Main supervised beaches

Les Grenettes at Sainte-Marie-de-Ré, the beaches at Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré, La Couarde's Atlantic beach, and Sablanceaux at Rivedoux are among the main supervised zones. The official list is published annually by each commune.

## Outside supervised zones

Away from lifeguard posts and outside peak season, no flags are raised and each swimmer is responsible for their own assessment. On the Atlantic coast, currents and wave sets can be deceptive. Check the marine weather forecast before entering the water.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are all beaches supervised in summer?**
No. Only the most frequented beaches have lifeguard cover, primarily in July-August. Wild beaches (western coast, Les Portes) are never supervised.

**What do the swimming flags mean?**
Green: authorised and supervised. Yellow: supervised but with hazards. Red: swimming prohibited.
