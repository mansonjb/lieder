Le Goisil is one of the Atlantic coast's quieter beaches — wild, windswept and appreciated by those who know it rather than the crowds.

## Location and character

On the western facade of the island, between La Couarde and Ars-en-Ré, the Goisil beach is backed by low dunes and heath. The sand is pale and fine, often sculpted by the wind. The horizon is clear — nothing between you and the Americas.

## Swimming conditions

The Atlantic coast here is exposed. Wind-driven swell and currents make it less suitable for very young children in all but the calmest conditions. No supervision. Check conditions before entering.

## Who comes here

Nature walkers, photographers, people who've had enough of the busier beaches. Cyclists using the coastal route. Surfers in the right conditions.

## Access

Best by bike from either Loix or Ars-en-Ré. Some car access but parking is limited and paths can be sandy. No facilities on site — bring everything.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Le Goisil safe for swimming?**
In calm conditions and with care. Not supervised. Check the marine forecast first, especially with children.

**Is it possible to reach Le Goisil without a bike?**
Difficult without a car. By bike it's very accessible from either Loix or Ars.
