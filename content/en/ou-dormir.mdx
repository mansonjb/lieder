Accommodation on the Île de Ré ranges from campsites to boutique hotels, with the majority of visitors staying in rented houses and apartments.

## Types of accommodation

**Holiday rentals** — the dominant option. Houses and apartments rented by the week, typically with a garden. Platforms like Airbnb, Vrbo and direct rental agencies all operate here. Best booked 6–9 months ahead for July-August.

**Campsites** — the island has excellent campsites, some with direct beach access. La Couarde and Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré have the most. Well-equipped, often with swimming pools and facilities.

**Hotels** — limited but quality. Saint-Martin-de-Ré and La Flotte have the best-known hotel options. Generally boutique scale, not chains.

**Bed and breakfasts** (chambres d'hôtes) — available throughout the island, typically in converted farmhouses or village homes.

## When to book

For July and August, book as early as January. Spring and early autumn are more flexible. Off-season (October to April) can often be arranged a few weeks ahead.

## By village

Each village has its own accommodation page with more detail. Saint-Martin and La Flotte for the most character. Le Bois-Plage and La Couarde for the best beach access. Ars and Loix for the most authenticity.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is it cheaper to stay in the villages further from the bridge?**
Generally yes. Prices tend to rise with proximity to Saint-Martin. Ars-en-Ré and Les Portes are often cheaper for equivalent quality.

**Do hotels include breakfast?**
Some do, some don't. Confirm at booking. Many visitors prefer to buy from the local bakery regardless.
