Eating well on the Île de Ré is straightforward — the island's produce is outstanding, and local restaurants generally know how to use it.

## What to eat

**Oysters** — Ré oysters are among the finest in France. Best eaten at a waterside shack with bread, butter and a glass of Muscadet or Entre-Deux-Mers.

**Potatoes** — the Bonnotte de Noirmoutier often gets the headlines, but Ré potatoes (AOP) are outstanding. First of the season in May-June.

**Salt** — fleur de sel harvested in the island's marshes is one of the great French condiments. Sold everywhere; worth bringing home.

**Seafood** — whelks, cockles, crab, mussels and whatever the boats brought in that morning feature on most menus.

## Where to eat

Saint-Martin has the broadest choice. La Flotte has the best port-side dining. Ars-en-Ré is good value with a local feel. Most other villages have a handful of seasonal restaurants that work in season and close in winter.

## Budget

Set menus (formules) at lunch are the best value, often 15–22€ for two or three courses. Evening à la carte can run to 40–60€ per head at the better places.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do restaurants accept walk-ins in summer?**
The best places fill fast in July and August. Reserve two or three days ahead for dinner, especially in Saint-Martin and La Flotte.

**Are there options for vegetarians?**
Yes, increasingly so. The island's vegetable and salad produce is excellent, and most restaurants offer non-fish, non-meat options.
