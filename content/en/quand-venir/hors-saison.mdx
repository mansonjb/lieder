Off-season (October to April) on the Île de Ré is an increasingly popular choice for those who want the island without the summer context.

## What off-season means

The tourist infrastructure reduces dramatically. Quieter — genuinely quiet. The island's permanent community is more visible. Prices are significantly lower.

## Off-season pleasures

Winter walking on empty beaches. Bird reserve without crowds. The lighthouse without a queue. Markets that serve the locals, not the tourists. A slower rhythm.

## Who it suits

Couples, solo walkers, nature enthusiasts, those who've visited in summer and want to see another side of the island.

## Practical considerations

Confirm restaurant and shop opening before relying on them. Saint-Martin is the most reliable base for services year-round.

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the cheapest time to visit?**
January and February. Accommodation prices drop dramatically and availability is wide open.

**Is off-season accommodation easy to find?**
Very easy — some advance planning, but nothing like the summer booking scramble.
