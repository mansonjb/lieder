Summer on the Île de Ré (June to September) covers a range of very different experiences — from the uncrowded warmth of early June to the peak intensity of mid-August.

## The summer arc

**June:** Best combination of warmth, long days and manageable crowds.
**July:** Full peak season. Hot, busy, every service available.
**August:** Peak of peak. Maximum crowds, maximum prices, maximum energy.
**September:** The crowd drops, the light changes, the value improves.

## Swimming season

The sea warms to 18–19°C by late June, peaks at 21–22°C in August, stays pleasant through mid-September.

## Summer booking

For July-August: book accommodation in January or earlier. For June and September: 2–3 months ahead is usually sufficient.

## Frequently asked questions

**When in summer should I come for the best experience?**
June for the uncrowded version with long evenings. Early September for value and warmth without crowds.

**Is the island pleasant in August despite the crowds?**
Yes — the energy is real. August has an atmosphere that other months don't. Manage expectations about queues and costs.
