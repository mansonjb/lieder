Swimming on the Île de Ré is at its best from late June through September, with distinct conditions on each coastline.

## When to swim

**June:** Possible on warmer days, water 16–18°C. Best on pertuis side.
**July-August:** Peak season, 19–22°C, supervised beaches, ideal conditions.
**September:** Still very good, 19–21°C, less crowded.
**October:** For hardy swimmers, 17–18°C in early October.

## Atlantic vs pertuis

The Atlantic side has waves, stronger currents and cooler water. It's more physically demanding and more exciting. The pertuis side is calmer, shallower and warmer — better for children and relaxed swimming.

## Safety

Supervised beaches have flag systems (green/yellow/red). Follow them. On unsupervised Atlantic beaches, check conditions and respect your own ability level.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is it safe to swim alone on Atlantic beaches?**
With experience and in good conditions: yes. Never alone in poor conditions, regardless of experience.

**What's the best family swimming beach?**
Les Grenettes (Sainte-Marie-de-Ré) and Sablanceaux (Rivedoux-Plage) on the pertuis side — calm, shallow, supervised in season.
