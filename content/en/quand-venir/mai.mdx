May is one of the best months to visit the Île de Ré — the season is open, the island is not yet crowded, and the produce is exceptional.

## Why May is excellent

The island's AOP potatoes arrive in May — a seasonal event worth experiencing. Oysters are at their spring best. The hollyhocks begin to climb the walls. The cycle paths are empty compared to summer. Water is warming (15–17°C) — brave swimmers enjoy it.

## Public holidays

May has several French public holidays (1st May, 8th May, Ascension, sometimes Pentecost). These long weekends attract more visitors than a typical May week, but nothing compared to summer.

## Services

Full season operation by mid-May. Most restaurants and shops open.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is May warm enough for swimming?**
For hardy swimmers: yes. For children: the pertuis side is warmer and calmer if conditions allow. A wetsuit extends the season.

**What's happening in May that's worth experiencing?**
The first potato harvest and the market arrival of the season's earliest produce.
