January on the Île de Ré is genuinely quiet — the island belongs to its 18,000 permanent residents, and the experience is entirely different from summer.

## What January is like

Cold (5–9°C), sometimes stormy, the Atlantic at its most dramatic. The light is grey and horizontal, beautiful in its own way. The island is not closed — bakeries, some restaurants and basic services continue.

## Why come in January

For those who like winter landscapes, the island's marshes and wild beaches in January light are extraordinary. Bird populations peak. Salt marsh colour changes with the season. Complete peace.

## What's closed

Most tourist restaurants, shops and all the seasonal infrastructure. A village like Loix or Les Portes is essentially closed as a visitor destination.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Île de Ré worth visiting in January?**
For the right person — lovers of quiet, wild winter landscapes and authentic local life — yes. Not for beach holidays.

**Is the bridge open in January?**
Yes, year-round.
