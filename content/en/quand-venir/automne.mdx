Autumn (October-November) on the Île de Ré has specific pleasures that summer never offers — the salt harvest, the migration spectacle and the island's most dramatic Atlantic light.

## The autumn arc

**October:** Salt harvest, peak bird migration, half the tourist season still open.
**November:** Off-season, local, dramatic weather, minimal services.

## What autumn offers

The Lilleau des Niges reserve in October-November is at its most spectacular for migration. The light in autumn is distinctive — lower angle, warmer colour, longer shadows.

## Cycling in autumn

The cycle paths are empty. Autumn cycling between the villages, through the marshes, to the lighthouse — excellent without the summer crowds.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is swimming possible in October?**
The first half of October, sea temperatures are still 17–18°C — possible for those with some cold tolerance.

**What's the bird spectacle in autumn specifically?**
Thousands of waders moving through on the Atlantic flyway — dunlins, knots, godwits, sandpipers — alongside the resident egrets, herons and marsh harriers.
