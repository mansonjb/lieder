French public holidays create long weekends (ponts) that generate significant short-stay traffic to the island. Planning around them is worthwhile.

## Key long weekends

**May 1 (Labour Day):** First major influx of the season. The island is warm, flowers are out, and many French families use this long weekend for a preview.

**May 8 (Victory in Europe Day):** Similar — another April-May preview weekend.

**Ascension (variable, May):** 4-day weekend, busy.

**Pentecost (variable, May-June):** One of the most significant long weekends, often driving the real start of tourist season traffic.

**Bastille Day (July 14):** In full season, any additional traffic is felt.

**Assumption (August 15):** Peak of peak. The busiest single weekend of the year.

## Strategy

If you're not visiting specifically for a long weekend, plan arrival and departure to avoid the Saturday changeover and holiday peaks.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Bastille Day celebrated on the island?**
Yes — fireworks typically at Saint-Martin-de-Ré on the evening of July 13 or 14.

**Should I add days to a long weekend to spread the cost?**
Often yes — a Tuesday-to-Sunday stay can be cheaper and less crowded than a standard Saturday-to-Saturday.
