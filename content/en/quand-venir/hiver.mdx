Winter on the Île de Ré (November to March) is a completely different experience from summer — quiet, authentic and beautiful in ways that summer visitors never see.

## Winter character

Cold (5–12°C), variable weather, the Atlantic at its most dramatic. The island belongs to its 18,000 year-round residents. Many tourist facilities close.

## Why come in winter

The bird reserve at Lilleau des Niges is at its most species-rich. The salt marshes have their winter character. The village streets are uncrowded. The lighthouse can be visited without queuing. The island's real personality is visible.

## Practical winter visit

A self-catering rental in Saint-Martin or Le Bois-Plage gives access to year-round services. A few reliable restaurants continue through winter.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is winter on Île de Ré depressing?**
For the wrong person: yes. For those who like dramatic empty landscapes and authentic local life: it's one of the best times.

**What's open in winter?**
Year-round essentials in Saint-Martin (bakery, pharmacy, supermarket, some restaurants). Limited elsewhere.
