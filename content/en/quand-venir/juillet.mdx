July is the island's peak month. Everything is open, the weather is typically excellent, and the island is at full capacity. For British families, July is when UK schools break up: the last two weeks of July see the largest influx of British visitors of the year.

## July character

Hot and sunny most days (24 to 27 degrees average). Sea temperature 20 to 21 degrees. All beaches supervised. Every restaurant, shop and service operating. Markets at maximum. The evening light lasts until well past ten.

## British school holidays in July

UK state schools typically break for summer in the third or fourth week of July, with most finishing between 18 and 25 July. The first full week of the British school holidays is one of the busiest periods on the island. If you have flexibility, aim for early July (before UK schools finish) or August.

For British families: if you must travel in peak school holiday time, book as early as possible, the previous November at the latest for the best properties. Ferry crossings on the Brittany Ferries Portsmouth to La Rochelle route fill up quickly for late July.

## Crowds

The island's population multiplies from 18,000 to over 100,000. Traffic on the main road, queues at the bridge (especially Saturday afternoons and Sunday evenings), packed beaches in the afternoon. This is real, but the island manages it reasonably well. Beaches are wide. Cycle paths are separated from traffic. Villages have space.

The worst times for congestion: Saturday 14:00 to 18:00 (arrivals), Sunday 15:00 to 20:00 (departures), and any wet afternoon when everyone moves indoors.

## What is still worth it

July has something no other month can replicate: the specific energy of a French island in full summer. The markets at peak. The evening atmosphere at Saint-Martin port. Every restaurant open, every bar lively, oyster shacks busy at lunch. If you want the island fully alive, July delivers that.

## Booking

If you have not booked July accommodation by February, your options are severely limited. Top villas and hotels at Toiras or Le Richelieu often sell out months earlier than this for peak July. Book as soon as your dates are confirmed.

Ferry bookings on the Portsmouth to La Rochelle route: book as early as Brittany Ferries opens the schedule (typically the autumn prior).

## Frequently asked questions

**Is July too crowded to enjoy?**
It depends on your crowd tolerance. The island manages its peak reasonably well. But it is genuinely busy, and the bridge queue on a Saturday afternoon can be frustrating. Come with realistic expectations.

**What is the best time of day in July to avoid crowds?**
Early morning (before 10am) for beaches, markets and villages. Late evening (after 8pm) for restaurants and the port atmosphere.

**Is July better or worse than August for British visitors?**
Early July (before UK schools break) is noticeably quieter than August. Once UK schools finish (around 18 to 25 July), the island becomes very busy for the rest of the summer.

**Should we pay more for July accommodation or just visit in September?**
Both are valid choices. September has better value and quieter beaches, but fewer things are open. July has full energy and higher prices. For a first visit with children, July is probably worth the premium.
