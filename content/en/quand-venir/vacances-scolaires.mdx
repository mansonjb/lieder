French school holiday periods drive the island's busiest weeks. Understanding them helps plan around or within them.

## The critical periods

**July and August:** The entire school summer holidays. The island's main season. All weeks are high season.

**Spring school holidays (April):** A busy period — Easter and spring break overlap here. Hotels and rentals fill.

**Christmas/New Year:** Some families come, but the island remains relatively quiet.

## Zone differences

French schools have three zones (A, B, C) with slightly different holiday dates. Zone B (including La Rochelle's region) affects local demand most directly.

## Implications for visitors

Booking during non-school-holiday periods — early June, September, October — gives the best balance of open services and manageable crowds.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are the school spring holidays as busy as summer?**
Significantly less so. Busy compared to non-holiday periods, but nothing like July-August.

**Is the island open at Christmas for families?**
Partially. The main services in Saint-Martin and Le Bois-Plage are open; most smaller establishments are closed.
