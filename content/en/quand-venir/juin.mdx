June is often cited as the best month on the Île de Ré. Long evenings, increasingly warm weather, uncrowded beaches and the full bloom of the hollyhocks.

## Why June is often best

The sun sets after 9:30pm at midsummer. The island is busy but not overwhelmed. Water temperature reaches 17–19°C — good for swimming. All services are operating. The flowers are at their peak.

## Crowds

Busy on weekends and public holidays; relatively manageable on weekdays before mid-June. The last two weeks of June start to feel like summer in terms of occupancy.

## Value

Prices are typically 20–30% below peak (July-August) for equivalent accommodation. Better availability.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is June worth the extra cost compared to May?**
If swimming is a priority, the warmer water justifies the slightly higher cost. In May, beach walks are lovely but swimming is for the brave.

**Are school groups present in June?**
French school year ends in late June. Early June can see some school trips; from July it's mainly families.
