The weather on the Île de Ré is shaped by the Atlantic — milder than inland France, but with more wind and cloud variation.

## The climate

Maritime temperate. Mild winters (rarely below 5°C), warm rather than hot summers (25–27°C average). Sunshine hours are among the highest on France's Atlantic coast. Wind is a constant presence, especially on the exposed western coast.

## Rainfall

The island receives around 600–700mm of annual rainfall, distributed through the year with slightly more in winter. Summer can have extended dry spells but rain is always possible.

## Wind

Prevailing south-westerlies. The Atlantic side of the island is more exposed. Even on sunny days, a light windproof is useful on the western beaches.

## Fog

Early morning summer fog (stratus) occasionally affects the island, burning off by mid-morning.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Île de Ré hotter than Paris in summer?**
Temperatures are similar. The island feels cooler due to sea breezes. It's a more comfortable heat.

**Is the weather reliable in June?**
Mostly — June is generally good, but the Atlantic can surprise. Packing a waterproof is always sensible.
