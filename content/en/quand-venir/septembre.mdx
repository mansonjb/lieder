September is many regular visitors' favourite month on the Île de Ré — warm, beautiful light, quieter and less expensive.

## September character

Water temperature stays at 19–21°C through mid-September (excellent swimming). Air temperature typically 20–24°C. The golden light of early autumn. The crowds drop sharply from the first week.

## Opening situation

Most restaurants and shops continue through September. The full season service persists until around 20 September, then some seasonal businesses close.

## Value

Prices drop 20–40% from August peak. Availability improves from the first week. Better value than any summer month.

## Autumn migration

September is one of the best months for birdwatching at Lilleau des Niges — the autumn migration of shorebirds and waders through the reserve is outstanding.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is swimming still good in September?**
Yes — sea temperatures remain excellent through mid-September, dropping to 17–18°C by month's end.

**Can I find accommodation a few weeks ahead in September?**
Generally yes, unlike July-August where months of lead time is required.
