August is the height of French summer, and the Île de Ré is at its busiest, most expensive and most vibrant.

## August character

The warmest month of the year — sea temperatures at 21–22°C, air often 26–30°C. Long beach days, full restaurant terraces, evening promenades.

## The crowd situation

August is genuinely very busy. The bridge can back up for hours on Saturday changeover days (the main rental changeover). Book accommodation with 6+ months lead time.

## What to do

Everything. Every service, every restaurant, all water sports, all tours. August is the island fully open.

## Tips for August visitors

Arrive on a weekday rather than Saturday. Choose beaches on the less-visited western end. Eat lunch at 11:45am or 2:30pm to avoid peak restaurant queues.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is August better or worse than July?**
Slightly hotter, slightly more crowded, similar in character. July is marginally better for availability; August can feel more festive.

**Is 15 August especially busy?**
Yes — the Feast of the Assumption is a major holiday in France. The bridge and island are at maximum capacity around this weekend.
