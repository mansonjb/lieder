Ten communes share the Île de Ré. Each has a distinct identity — the island is small enough to visit several in a day by bike, but different enough that your choice of base matters.

## The villages at a glance

[Rivedoux-Plage](/en/villages/rivedoux-plage) — first stop after the bridge, practical and well-served, good family beaches.

[Sainte-Marie-de-Ré](/en/villages/sainte-marie-de-re) — large, lively, close to the bridge, popular with families, good kitesurfing at La Cible beach.

[La Flotte](/en/villages/la-flotte) — charming port village, flower-lined streets, one of France's most beautiful villages, excellent market.

[Saint-Martin-de-Ré](/en/villages/saint-martin-de-re) — the island's main town, fortified by Vauban, UNESCO World Heritage, most restaurants and nightlife.

[Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré](/en/villages/le-bois-plage-en-re) — central, great Atlantic beaches, well-developed tourism infrastructure.

[La Couarde-sur-Mer](/en/villages/la-couarde-sur-mer) — central position, access to both coastlines, popular with families.

[Loix](/en/villages/loix) — remote peninsula, salt marshes, very quiet, authentic.

[Ars-en-Ré](/en/villages/ars-en-re) — village of salt workers, black church spire, nature reserve nearby, one of France's most beautiful villages.

[Saint-Clément-des-Baleines](/en/villages/saint-clement-des-baleines) — westernmost village before Les Portes, home to the lighthouse.

[Les Portes-en-Ré](/en/villages/les-portes-en-re) — the wild end of the island, minimal infrastructure, maximum tranquility.

## How to choose

If this is your first time, Saint-Martin or La Flotte gives you the most convenient base with the most to do on foot. Families with young children often prefer La Couarde or Le Bois-Plage for the beaches. Those looking for peace and nature should head to Ars, Loix or Les Portes.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which village is the most lively?**
Saint-Martin-de-Ré, by some distance. Restaurants, bars, shops, evening activity — it has the most.

**Which village is the quietest?**
Loix or Les Portes-en-Ré, depending on the season. Both are deliberately low-key.
