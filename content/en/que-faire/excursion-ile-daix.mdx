Île d'Aix is a small, car-free island 25 minutes by ferry from Fouras on the mainland or via some Île de Ré boat operators. A day trip from Ré makes for an excellent contrast.

## What is Île d'Aix

A tiny island (3km long, 600m wide) with a population of about 200. No cars. Napoleon spent his last French days here before his final exile to Saint-Helena. Beautiful beaches, a historic fort, a village of extraordinary peace.

## From Île de Ré

Some operators run combined trips from Saint-Martin-de-Ré to Île d'Aix. Alternatively, take a boat from Fouras on the mainland (30-minute drive from the bridge).

## What to do on Île d'Aix

Walk the full perimeter in 2 hours. Visit the Napoleon museum. Eat at one of the few village restaurants. Lie on a beach. That's the programme. Bring a picnic.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Île d'Aix worth a day trip from Île de Ré?**
Yes, particularly for those interested in Napoleon history or who want to experience an even smaller, quieter island.

**Can you get to Île d'Aix without a car?**
Yes — ferry from Fouras. From the Île de Ré, some operators provide a direct service.
