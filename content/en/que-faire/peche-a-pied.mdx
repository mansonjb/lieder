Shore fishing (pêche à pied) is a long island tradition — heading out at low tide to gather shellfish from the exposed mudflats. Accessible, free and directly rewarding.

## When and where

Best during spring tides (coefficient above 90) on the northern and eastern coast. Le Boutillon at Loix, the coastal areas near Lilleau des Niges, and the La Flotte shoreline are productive.

## What to bring

Old shoes or wellies. A bucket or net bag. A small rake for cockles in sandy areas. The Marée.info app for tide predictions.

## What you'll find

Cockles, periwinkles, small crabs, occasionally clams in sandy zones. The yield and variety depend on the specific habitat.

## Rules

Minimum sizes apply (cockles: 2cm, clams: 4cm). Maximum personal quantity: 5kg per person per day. Commercial harvesting is strictly prohibited.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do children enjoy pêche à pied?**
Very much — it's one of the island's best family activities. The discovery element of what's under each stone or in the sand engages children well.

**Is it safe to eat what you find?**
Yes, in authorised zones. Avoid collecting near any obvious pollution sources and check with locals if uncertain.
