The Vauban fortifications of Saint-Martin-de-Ré are the island's outstanding architectural heritage and a UNESCO World Heritage site.

## What they are

Designed by Sébastien Le Prestre de Vauban, Louis XIV's master military engineer, the fortifications were built and extended between 1681 and 1696. They encircle Saint-Martin-de-Ré completely and remain one of Vauban's most intact works.

## The walking circuit

You can walk the full perimeter of the ramparts — approximately 2.5 km — on the top of the walls. This takes about 45 minutes at a relaxed pace, with excellent views over the port, the town and the surrounding countryside.

## The citadel

The citadel within the fortifications was designed to be the town's last refuge in case of attack. A portion is still in use as a prison — one of France's oldest still-functioning. Limited access; the exterior and context are visible from the ramparts circuit.

## Context

Saint-Martin's fortifications are among 12 Vauban sites across France listed as UNESCO World Heritage in 2008, under the "Fortifications of Vauban" designation.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the rampart walk free?**
Yes — the perimeter walk is free and open at all times.

**How long does the full Vauban heritage visit take?**
The rampart walk plus time in the town: 2–3 hours for a satisfying visit.
