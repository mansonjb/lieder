The Lilleau des Niges nature reserve occupies the northern tip of the Île de Ré and is one of France's most significant migratory bird staging grounds.

## What it is

A coastal wetland reserve of approximately 300 hectares, encompassing salt marshes, mudflats and dune habitats. It's managed by the LPO (Ligue pour la Protection des Oiseaux — France's leading bird conservation body).

## What you'll see

The reserve hosts over 300 bird species across the year. Migratory shorebirds in spring and autumn — spoonbills, godwits, dunlins, sandpipers, terns — make it particularly rewarding in April-May and August-October. Resident species include egrets, herons, marsh harriers and various waders.

## Access

Open year-round via marked paths from the northern road near Ars-en-Ré. Free access to the trails. Guided tours operate in season (book via the LPO or tourism office).

## Equipment

Binoculars are a significant enhancement. Some hide structures along the paths. Wellies or waterproof shoes recommended outside summer.

## Frequently asked questions

**When is the best season for birdwatching at Lilleau des Niges?**
April-May and September-October for migration. Year-round for resident species.

**Is the reserve accessible to children?**
Yes — the flat, easy terrain and the birdwatching subject matter engage many children well.
