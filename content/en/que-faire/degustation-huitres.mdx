Oysters from the Île de Ré are among France's finest. Eating them properly — fresh from the local beds, on ice, with good bread and a glass of white — is one of the island's essential experiences.

## The oysters

Ré oysters are primarily flat and cupped oysters from the local beds in the pertuis charentais. The combination of Atlantic ocean water and the calmer pertuis conditions produces a specific flavour: briny but clean, with mineral character.

## Where to eat them

**Cabanes ostréicoles** — the oyster shacks are the most direct experience. Some sell direct at the water's edge, opening the oysters to order.

**La Flotte market** — one of the island's best places to eat oysters at the market, standing up with a glass of white.

**Port restaurants** — most restaurants in La Flotte and Saint-Martin serve oysters as a starter.

## How to order

Ask for huîtres, specify the number (a dozen or half-dozen), and if you have a preference: creuses (cupped) or plates (flat). Both are excellent.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are Île de Ré oysters available year-round?**
Yes, though the traditional rule of "months with an R" (September to April) reflects the ideal conditions. Summer oysters are fine from certified beds with refrigeration.

**What wine goes with Île de Ré oysters?**
A dry white: Muscadet, Entre-Deux-Mers, or a dry Charentes wine.
