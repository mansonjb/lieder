Kitesurfing on the Île de Ré is centred on La Cible beach at Sainte-Marie-de-Ré, which has the conditions and infrastructure for both learning and progressive riding.

## La Cible

Consistent south-westerly wind, extensive shallow water, a kitesurfing school with qualified instructors. It's the natural home of the sport on the island.

## Learning

Complete beginner courses typically run 3–5 hours over one or two days, starting on the beach before entering the water. Students progress from body-dragging to board riding. Safety equipment provided.

## Beyond La Cible

Experienced riders explore other sections of the Atlantic coast in appropriate wind conditions.

## Conditions

Summer months can be light on wind — early morning and late afternoon tend to be windiest. September is often excellent. Ask the school about current forecasts.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does it take to learn kitesurfing?**
Most people can ride independently after 10–15 hours of instruction. La Cible's conditions accelerate learning compared to deeper water sites.

**What's the minimum age for kitesurfing lessons?**
Most schools accept students from 12–14 upward, depending on body weight and physical development.
