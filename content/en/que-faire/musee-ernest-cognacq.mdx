The Musée Ernest Cognacq in Saint-Martin-de-Ré is the island's main museum — covering its history, salt trade, natural heritage and maritime past in a former town house.

## What it covers

Named after the merchant Ernest Cognacq (born on the island, who later co-founded La Samaritaine in Paris), the museum's collections span: island natural history, the salt trade and its tools, maritime heritage, the history of the fortifications, and island art.

## The building

A 17th-century townhouse near the church, the building itself is part of the experience — vaulted ceilings and period rooms give context to the collections.

## Practical information

Entry fee. Seasonal opening hours (open most of the year; check for current times). Located in the centre of Saint-Martin.

## Frequently asked questions

**How long does the museum visit take?**
About 1–1.5 hours for a thorough visit.

**Is it suitable for children?**
The salt and maritime sections are accessible to children. The museum overall is more rewarding for adults.
