Kayaking on the Île de Ré opens up the island's coastline in a way that walking and cycling cannot — the pertuis shallows, the salt marsh channels and the open bays all look different from the water.

## Where to kayak

The pertuis charentais (southern, sheltered coast) is the main kayaking zone — calm water, no significant current in most conditions, interesting coastal scenery. The salt marsh channels near Loix and Ars can be explored at high tide. The Atlantic side is for experienced paddlers only.

## Hire

Kayak hire is available at several beaches in season. Stand-up paddleboards also available. Half-day and full-day hire. Guided tours with a local instructor are a good option for first-timers.

## What to see

From the water: the coastline looks entirely different. Old coastal defences, oyster beds, birds working the shallows, the salt pan landscapes seen from sea level.

## Conditions

Check tide times before going out — tidal currents can be significant in the channels. The pertuis side is generally manageable for beginners in good weather.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do you need experience to kayak on the island?**
Not for the pertuis side in calm conditions. Basic paddling ability is helpful but not essential with a hire and brief instruction.

**Can you kayak with children?**
Yes — tandem kayaks are available for hire. Children from about 5–6 years can ride as second paddler.
