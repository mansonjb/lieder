Beyond the postcard highlights, the Île de Ré has experiences that require more local knowledge but reward it well.

## Less-obvious things to do

**Photograph Loix from a bicycle at dawn** — the flat light over the marshes, the village appearing at the end of an empty road.

**Watch the paludiers work in July heat** — the salt harvest is a genuinely remarkable thing to observe.

**Take the coastal path at extreme low tide** — the full extent of the mudflats, birds working across exposed kilometres of seafloor.

**Visit the cemetery at Ars-en-Ré** — beautifully maintained, distinctly Atlantic in character, with headstones telling the history of the island's fishing and seafaring families.

**Eat at the fish market in Saint-Martin at 7am** — the real market, for the real customers, before the tourist world wakes up.

**Take the bridge on foot** — it's 3km, takes 45 minutes, and gives you a view of the island from outside that you can't get any other way.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you walk the bridge?**
Yes, there's a pedestrian and cycle path alongside the road. Worth doing once.

**When does the morning fish market run in Saint-Martin?**
Very early — arrive before 8am. Seasonal; check locally.
