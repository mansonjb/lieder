The Île de Ré is one of France's premier birdwatching destinations, combining a major migratory route with exceptional wetland habitats.

## Why Île de Ré for birds

The island sits on the East Atlantic Flyway — the main migration route for millions of birds moving between northern breeding grounds and African wintering areas. Its wetlands (salt marshes, mudflats, reed beds) provide critical staging habitat.

## Key sites

**Lilleau des Niges nature reserve** — the headline site. Open year-round, free access, guided tours in season.

**Salt marshes around Loix and Ars** — excellent for waders and egrets year-round.

**Coastal pools and scrapes** — various locations across the island support diverse wetland birds.

## What you might see

Spoonbills, avocets, black-winged stilts, various herons and egrets, marsh harriers, numerous wader species in migration, terns, gulls. Winter brings ducks and geese in the marshes.

## Practical tips

Binoculars are essential. Dawn and dusk are most productive. Spring (April-May) and autumn (August-October) migration periods are the peaks.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do you need to book for guided tours?**
Recommended for the Lilleau des Niges guided tours in July-August. Contact the LPO or tourism office.

**Is the Île de Ré good for birdwatching beginners?**
Very — the variety and accessibility of the sites, combined with the number of easily identified species, makes it rewarding for all experience levels.
