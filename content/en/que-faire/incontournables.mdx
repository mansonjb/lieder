Every first-time visitor to the Île de Ré should tick off these experiences — the island's essential highlights.

## The must-dos

**1. Walk the Saint-Martin ramparts** — free, always open, the best 45-minute introduction to the island's history.

**2. Eat oysters at La Flotte market** — the island's quintessential gastronomy experience.

**3. Cycle to Ars-en-Ré and back through the marshes** — the best one-day cycling route for scenery and atmosphere.

**4. Visit the Phare des Baleines** — climb it at sunset for the Atlantic view.

**5. Spend a morning at a beach on each coastline** — understand the island's two distinct faces.

**6. Buy fleur de sel** — either direct from a paludier or at the market. It travels well and makes an excellent gift.

**7. Visit Loix** — understand what the island looks like when tourism hasn't arrived.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is one week enough to do all of these?**
Comfortably. Most can be combined — the cycling routes connect multiple highlights.

**Which of these requires advance booking?**
The lighthouse can have queues in peak season — arrive early or late. Everything else is walk-in.
