The Abbaye des Châteliers is a 12th-century Cistercian abbey ruin standing in the fields between La Flotte and Rivedoux — one of the island's most peaceful and photogenic historic sites.

## History

Founded in the 12th century, the abbey was one of the most powerful Cistercian establishments on the Atlantic coast. It was progressively destroyed during the Wars of Religion and the Hundred Years War, and the ruins now stand as a romantic remnant.

## The site today

Free access year-round. The ruins consist of stone arches and walls in various states of preservation, set in open farmland. No café, no entry fee, no formal visitor infrastructure — just the ruins and the sky.

## Getting there

By bike from La Flotte: 5 minutes. On foot: 15–20 minutes through the fields. From Rivedoux: 20 minutes by bike. Easy to combine with a La Flotte market visit.

## When to visit

Late afternoon light gives the stone the most warmth. The ruins are particularly atmospheric in mist or dramatic weather.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there anything inside the abbey ruins?**
The ruins are open-air — walls and arches without a roof. You walk through and around them.

**Is the Abbaye des Châteliers well-signposted?**
Moderately. The best approach is to ask in La Flotte for directions or check a detailed local map.
