The Île de Ré is not a major wine-producing territory, but Charente and Charente-Maritime wines are very much part of the table culture.

## Local wines

The island itself has minimal vineyard land. The relevant regional production comes from the Charente-Maritime and Poitevin appellations on the mainland — typically dry whites, rosés and local spirits.

## Pineau des Charentes

A local specialty: a fortified wine made from unfermented grape juice and Cognac or Armagnac. Drunk as an aperitif, typically chilled. Available in white, rosé and red styles. Worth trying once.

## Where to find it

Local wine shops in Saint-Martin-de-Ré and the village markets. Restaurant wine lists will include regional options.

## Cognac and Armagnac

The Charente region is Cognac country. Bottles are available throughout the island for those interested in the broader regional spirit culture.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there a wine tasting experience available on Île de Ré?**
No dedicated winery tours on the island. For serious wine tourism, the Cognac cellars on the mainland (30–45 minutes by car) offer tours and tastings.

**What is the local aperitif culture on the island?**
Pineau des Charentes is the classic local aperitif. Muscadet or a local dry white is the standard with oysters.
