Île de Ré is one of France's best family holiday islands — the combination of flat cycling, calm beaches and varied activities suits children of all ages.

## For young children (under 8)

The pertuis beaches (Sablanceaux, Les Grenettes, La Couarde south) are calm and shallow — ideal for toddlers and early swimmers. Shore fishing (pêche à pied) at low tide is immediately engaging for young children. The cycle paths are safe and flat for beginner cyclists.

## For older children (8–14)

Surf lessons at Le Bois-Plage. Kayaking on the pertuis. The lighthouse climb. Fort Boyard boat trip. The full island cycling tour is achievable over two days.

## For teenagers

Kitesurfing at La Cible. Surf at Le Gros Jonc. The freedom of exploring on bikes independently.

## What works for everyone

Cycling between villages, the morning market ritual, beach days with good food.

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the minimum age for surf lessons?**
Most schools accept children from 6–7 years upward.

**Are there playgrounds on the island?**
Yes, in most villages. Ask locally.
