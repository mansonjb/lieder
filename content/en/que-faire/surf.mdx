Surfing on the Île de Ré is accessible, consistent and underrated — not a big-wave destination but an excellent place to learn or refine technique.

## The spots

Le Gros Jonc (Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré) is the main spot — beach break, consistent swell, large enough for many surfers simultaneously. Les Gollandières, Trousse-Chemise and the Les Portes beaches provide alternatives.

## Conditions

Best in autumn and spring when Atlantic low-pressure systems generate reliable swell. Summer can be flat or small. Wave height: typically 0.5–1.5m, occasionally 2m in autumn.

## Schools

Surf schools in Le Bois-Plage operate in season. Beginner lessons (surfboard, wetsuit, instruction included) from approximately €35–50 for a 2-hour session.

## Wetsuits

The Atlantic is cool — even in summer a 3/2mm wetsuit extends comfort. Wetsuits are included in most lesson packages and available for hire separately.

## Frequently asked questions

**What age can children learn to surf on Île de Ré?**
Most surf schools accept children from 6–7 years upward. Children often learn faster than adults.

**Is Île de Ré good for surfing in winter?**
The island gets its best surf conditions in autumn and winter, but infrastructure (schools, hire) largely closes after October.
