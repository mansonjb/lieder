The Île de Ré works very well for families with babies and toddlers — the flat terrain, calm beaches and relaxed pace make it manageable even with very young children.

## Best beaches

Sablanceaux at Rivedoux-Plage and Les Grenettes at Sainte-Marie-de-Ré are the calmest, shallowest options — perfect for toddler paddling. The water on the pertuis side warms earlier in the season.

## Cycling with small children

Bike trailers and child seats are widely available for hire. The flat cycle paths make family cycling with a toddler very manageable.

## Nap schedules

The island's gentle pace accommodates nap times naturally. Most village accommodation has space for cots (confirm when booking).

## Practical needs

Supermarkets in Saint-Martin, Sainte-Marie and Le Bois-Plage stock baby food, nappies and formula. Pharmacies in all main villages.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the island too hot for small children in August?**
It can be warm. The sea breeze helps. Early morning and late afternoon beach time avoids the midday heat.

**Can you hire pushchair-friendly bikes?**
Cargo bikes and wide-wheel bikes suitable for pushchair-style child carriers are available from some hire shops. Ask specifically when booking.
