Fort de la Prée is a well-preserved 17th-century coastal fortification on the eastern tip of the island, between La Flotte and Rivedoux.

## History

Built in the early 17th century during the reign of Louis XIII, the fort was designed to guard the eastern approach to the island. It's in better condition than many comparable fortifications and the exterior is accessible.

## Access

The fort is visible from the coastal path. The exterior and grounds can be visited; interior access depends on current management (check locally).

## Getting there

By bike from La Flotte (about 20 minutes) or Rivedoux (15 minutes) along the coastal path. A satisfying detour on a pertuis coast cycling day.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Fort de la Prée open to visitors?**
The exterior is accessible from the coastal path. Internal access varies — check at the La Flotte tourist office.

**Is it worth visiting alongside the Vauban fortifications in Saint-Martin?**
Different scale and context. Saint-Martin's fortifications are grander; Fort de la Prée is more intimate and accessible in a natural setting.
