Walking on the Île de Ré offers a different pace and perspective from cycling — closer to the ground, slower through the villages, and more intimate with the landscape.

## The coastal paths

The island's coastal GR trail and local PR routes follow the shoreline and marsh edges. The north coast path between Ars and Loix is particularly beautiful — flat, birdy, luminous.

## Village walking

Every village centre rewards a slow walk. Saint-Martin's ramparts (45 minutes), La Flotte's lanes and port area, Ars-en-Ré's streets and church square.

## Salt marsh trails

Several marked trails cross the salt pan landscape between the villages. Flat, easy walking, excellent for birdwatching.

## Distance

Most walking on the island is gentle — nothing exceeds 200m in elevation. A full day walk would cover 15–20 km without difficulty.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are there walking maps for Île de Ré?**
Yes — the tourist office in Saint-Martin sells 1:25,000 maps. The PR routes are marked on IGN maps available locally.

**Is walking less enjoyable than cycling on the island?**
Different. Cycling covers more ground; walking is more immersive in specific landscapes. Both have their place.
