Fort Boyard, the island fortress made famous by the eponymous French TV show, sits in the water between the Île de Ré and the Île d'Oléron. Boat trips from Saint-Martin-de-Ré allow you to see it up close.

## What it is

Fort Boyard is a 19th-century sea fort, built on a sandbank in the Pertuis d'Antioche. It took 60+ years to complete and was immediately obsolete. Its current fame comes entirely from the television game show filmed there since 1990.

## The boat trip

Several operators run trips from Saint-Martin-de-Ré port, typically passing close to the fort and sometimes combining with Île d'Aix. Duration: 2–4 hours depending on the itinerary.

## What to expect

The fort is impressive from the water — completely surrounded by sea, regularly lapped by waves. You cannot go inside (it's still used for filming and privately managed).

## Booking

Essential in July-August. Trips depart daily in season from Saint-Martin port. Buy tickets at the port or in advance online.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you visit Fort Boyard inside?**
No — it's not open to public visits. The boat trip goes close enough for good photos.

**Is the boat trip suitable for children?**
Yes — the boats are stable ferries, not small inflatables. The trip suits children who enjoy being on the water.
