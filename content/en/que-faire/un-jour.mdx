If you only have one day on the Île de Ré, here's how to use it well.

## The optimal programme

**Morning (9–12):** Cross the bridge and head straight to La Flotte. If it's market day (Wednesday or Saturday), do the market first. Otherwise, walk the port and have coffee. Then cycle to Saint-Martin-de-Ré for the ramparts — a 20-minute ride on flat paths.

**Midday (12–14):** Lunch in Saint-Martin. A dozen oysters at a port restaurant if they're in budget. Or a crêpe and a view.

**Afternoon (14–17):** Cycle west to Le Bois-Plage for an Atlantic beach — Le Gros Jonc is 30 minutes from Saint-Martin. Swim. Return.

**Evening:** If staying, dinner at La Flotte or Saint-Martin. If leaving, cross the bridge before the evening peak.

## What you'll have seen

Two coastlines, the island's main town, its most beautiful village, an Atlantic beach. A solid first Île de Ré day.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you do the lighthouse in a single day?**
If you start early and move efficiently, you can add the lighthouse — but it makes for a long day. Better saved for a second visit.

**Is one day enough to know if you want to return?**
Yes — one well-structured day gives a genuine impression of what a longer visit offers.
