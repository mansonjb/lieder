The markets of the Île de Ré are among its great pleasures — local, seasonal and alive with the island's produce.

## The main markets

**Saint-Martin-de-Ré** — the island's largest, running mornings most days in season. Wide range: produce, flowers, clothes, crafts, fish.

**La Flotte** — Wednesday and Saturday mornings. Smaller, more local in character. Outstanding for oysters, vegetables and bread.

**Ars-en-Ré** — Friday mornings in season. Local and genuine.

**Le Bois-Plage** — daily in season. Good local produce selection.

## What to buy

Oysters (eat them there or take home on ice), pommes de terre (AOP Île de Ré potatoes — first of the season in May), fleur de sel, local honey, vegetables, flowers.

## The experience

Go early (before 10am) for the best selection and least crowds. Bring cash — not all vendors take cards. A shopping bag or basket is more practical than a backpack.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do markets run in winter?**
Very limited winter market activity. The main season is April to October.

**Can you buy oysters at the market and eat them on site?**
Yes — most market oyster vendors open and serve them on the spot, often with bread and butter.
