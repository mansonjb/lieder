The Phare des Baleines (Lighthouse of the Whales) is the island's most recognisable landmark and most visited monument. At 57 metres, it marks the western tip of the Île de Ré.

## The lighthouse

Built in its current form in 1854, the lighthouse has guided Atlantic shipping since the 17th century. The original lighthouse (the Ancienne Tour) dates to 1682 and stands alongside it — one of the oldest surviving lighthouses in France.

## Climbing it

Entry fee applies. Seasonal opening hours (typically 10am–6pm in season, extended in summer, reduced in winter). The climb is 257 steps — steep but manageable. The view from the top: the full length of the island in one direction, open Atlantic in the other.

## The sunset

In summer, the lighthouse is open late to allow sunset viewing from the platform. The sun sets directly over the ocean from this vantage point — one of the Atlantic coast's most celebrated spectacles.

## Practical information

Car park at the lighthouse. Accessible by bike from Ars-en-Ré (25 minutes) or Saint-Clément village (10 minutes on foot).

## Frequently asked questions

**Do you need to book tickets in advance?**
In July-August, queues can be long. Arriving early (before 10am) or late (after 5pm) reduces waiting time.

**Is the lighthouse suitable for children?**
Yes, for children old enough to manage stairs. The climb is safe but requires reasonable stamina.
