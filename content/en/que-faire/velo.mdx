Cycling is the defining activity on the Île de Ré. The island's cycle path network, over 100 kilometres, is one of France's finest, and the flat terrain makes it accessible to all ages and abilities. For British visitors in particular, arriving by ferry with their own bikes has become a popular choice: load the bike in Portsmouth, cycle off the boat in La Rochelle, cross the bridge on the dedicated cycle path, and you are on the island.

## The network

The main east-west axis crosses the island from Rivedoux to Les Portes-en-Ré, approximately 30 km each way. Side routes lead into the salt marshes, along both coastlines and through the village centres. Every commune is connected. The network uses dedicated off-road paths for most of its length, with short on-road sections through villages.

The cycle path is separated from road traffic for the vast majority of its route. Traffic-free cycling for children is entirely feasible.

## Bringing your own bike from the UK

The Portsmouth to La Rochelle Brittany Ferries crossing accepts bikes. Arriving on your own bike lets you cycle the 8 km from La Rochelle port to the bridge and straight onto the island, with no hire shop queues or availability issues in peak season. This is particularly worthwhile for cycling holidays of a week or more.

Bikes can also travel on Eurostar to Paris and then on TGV services to La Rochelle, though this requires a reservation and the bike must be in a bag on Eurostar.

## Hiring a bike on the island

Hire shops are present in every village. Prices: typically 10 to 15 euros per day for a standard bike, more for electric bikes. Weekly rates offer significantly better value. In July and August, book ahead if possible, particularly for electric bikes and family tandems.

Hire shops generally open from Easter through to the end of September. Out of season, availability drops sharply.

## Suggested routes by duration

**Half day (15 to 20 km)**: Rivedoux to La Flotte along the coast, returning via the interior path. Flat, straightforward, passes two of the island's nicest villages and a sheltered harbour.

**Full day (40 to 50 km)**: From Saint-Martin-de-Ré west through the salt marshes to Ars-en-Ré, continuing to the lighthouse at Les Baleines. Return along the Atlantic coast path. Excellent for picnic stops.

**Two-day island circuit (60+ km each way)**: Full circumnavigation of the island. Achievable for fit cyclists; split overnight at Ars-en-Ré or Les Portes. The western tip around Les Baleines is the least visited and most striking part of the circuit.

## Electric bikes

Increasingly popular, particularly for families with children and for covering longer distances without arriving exhausted. Electric hire bikes are available from most hire shops. Worth reserving in advance in peak season.

## With children

The Île de Ré is one of France's best family cycling destinations. The dedicated, traffic-free paths, flat terrain and short distances between villages make it safe and manageable for children from around six or seven upwards. Carry seats and trailers are available from some hire shops.

## Cycling from the ferry

If you arrive on the Brittany Ferries Portsmouth to La Rochelle crossing with your own bike, the route from the port to the island is as follows: exit La Rochelle port heading west, follow the cycle path signs to the bridge (approximately 8 km, mostly flat). The bridge has a dedicated pedestrian and cycle path on its southern side. At Rivedoux-Plage on the island, you join the main island cycle path network.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the cycling on Île de Ré suitable for children?**
Yes. The flat terrain and dedicated paths make it one of France's best family cycling destinations. Children of seven or eight can manage 15 to 20 km comfortably on their own bikes.

**Can you bring your own bike via the Portsmouth to La Rochelle ferry?**
Yes. Brittany Ferries accepts bikes on the crossing. You can cycle from the La Rochelle port directly to the island bridge.

**Is it worth bringing your own bike rather than hiring?**
For a week or more, particularly if you are keen cyclists, yes. Hire bikes in peak season can be basic and availability can be limited. Your own bike, particularly a good touring bike or gravel bike, will handle the paths better.

**Are electric bikes available to hire?**
Yes, at most hire shops. Worth reserving ahead in July and August.
