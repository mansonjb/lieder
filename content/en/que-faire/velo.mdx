Cycling is the defining activity on the Île de Ré. The island's cycle path network — over 100 kilometres — is one of France's finest, and the flat terrain makes it accessible to all ages and abilities.

## The network

The main east-west axis crosses the island from Rivedoux to Les Portes-en-Ré, approximately 30 km each way. Side routes lead into the marshes, along the coasts and through the village centres. Every commune is connected.

## Hiring a bike

Hire shops in every village. Prices: typically €10–15 per day for a standard bike, more for electric bikes (which are worth considering if cycling with children). Hire by the day or week; weekly rates offer better value.

## What to ride

Day 1: Rivedoux to La Flotte and back. Day 2: La Couarde through the marshes to Ars. Day 3: West to the lighthouse and back. Full island loop: 60+ km, achievable in a day for fit cyclists.

## Electric bikes

Increasingly popular, particularly for families with children and those who want to cover more distance comfortably. Electric hire bikes are available from most hire shops.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is the cycling on Île de Ré suitable for children?**
Yes — the flat terrain and dedicated paths make it one of France's best family cycling destinations.

**Can you bring your own bike via train?**
Yes — bikes are allowed on TGV services (with a reservation), and La Rochelle has train access.
