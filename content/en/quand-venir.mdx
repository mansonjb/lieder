The Île de Ré is open year-round, but the experience changes dramatically by season.

## Peak season: July and August

The island is at its most alive — and most crowded. Beaches packed, restaurants full, traffic on the single main road. Book accommodation months ahead. Prices are at their highest. If this is your window, it's still worth it: the light is spectacular and the energy is real.

## Shoulder season: June and September

The best compromise. Water temperature is warm enough to swim comfortably (18–20°C). Crowds are manageable. Most restaurants are open. Prices are 20–40% lower than peak. June in particular has the longest days of the year.

## Spring: April and May

Flowers everywhere, cool but not cold, cycle paths empty. Some restaurants and shops are still closed in early April. The market at La Flotte is excellent. Bird migration peaks at Lilleau des Niges in May.

## Autumn: October and November

Wild, windy, beautiful. The Atlantic is dramatic. Most tourist infrastructure closes or reduces hours after mid-October. Good for walking and cycling without the crowds. The salt harvest is in October.

## Winter: December to March

Quiet and authentic. Permanent residents outnumber visitors. Most rental properties are closed. Some hotels operate year-round. The island's winter character is quite different from summer — worth experiencing once.

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the water temperature in summer?**
Around 18–20°C in July-August, dropping to 15–17°C in September, rising through June from 15°C.

**Can you swim in June?**
Yes. The Atlantic is invigorating at 16–18°C in June. Wetsuits optional depending on your cold tolerance.
