Île de Ré is an island of houses. No tower blocks, no generic complexes: low-rise homes, lime-washed walls, blue or green shutters, wisteria on the facades. Renting a villa here means settling into this setting for a week or two, with a garden, a bike in the hallway and a terrace for breakfast.

## What you'll find in terms of villas

The holiday rental market is large and varied. There are broadly two types of property:

**Village houses**: small, often renovated, tucked into the lanes of each commune. They typically have a courtyard or small garden and let you do everything on foot. Ideal for couples or groups of 4.

**Villas with garden and pool**: more spread out, often on the outskirts of villages or in residential areas. Larger (6 to 10 guests), with a private pool and terrace. The best choice for groups or family stays.

## Prices and seasons

In July and August, rates rise sharply. A decent village house for 4 starts at around 200 to 300 euros per night. A pool villa for 8 can easily reach 800 to 1200 euros per night in high season.

In June or September, the same properties rent for 30 to 50% less, and beaches are noticeably quieter.

## Book early

The best villas go fast, sometimes from November or December for the following summer. If you have fixed dates in July-August, don't wait.

## Pool or no pool

A pool can double the price. For families with young children or during a particularly hot week, it is worth it. For a couple or a short stay, a well-located villa without a pool in a beautiful village is often better value.
