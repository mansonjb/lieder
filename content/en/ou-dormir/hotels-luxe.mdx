Île de Ré is not a destination that prioritises luxury hotels. Its appeal is more elemental: light, cycling, oysters, beaches, white walls. But at the top end, a small number of properties offer genuinely exceptional experiences, and the island's relative simplicity makes them feel more special than they would in a more crowded market.

## Hotel de Toiras, Saint-Martin-de-Ré

The island's uncontested flagship property. A 17th-century mansion converted into a 5-star Relais and Chateaux hotel, set within the Vauban fortifications in Saint-Martin. Fifteen rooms and suites, each different. An indoor pool, a spa, a gastronomic restaurant. The service is exceptional by any standard, the setting remarkable.

Rates typically start around 300 to 400 euros per night in season, considerably more for suites. This is serious luxury.

**Best for**: couples, anniversary trips, honeymoons. Anyone who wants the best the island can offer in hotel terms.

## La Baronnie Hotel and Spa, Saint-Martin-de-Ré

A 4-star property within the fortifications of Saint-Martin, with a pool and spa. More accessible than Toiras in price, still at the high end of the island's accommodation offer. Good spa facilities and a pleasant garden.

Rates from around 180 to 280 euros per night in season.

**Best for**: couples who want a good spa experience without the full Toiras price point.

## Le Richelieu, La Flotte

A 4-star hotel with sea views and direct beach access at La Flotte. Reliable, well-managed, with a pool and a decent restaurant. This is the island's best hotel with genuine beach access. La Flotte itself is one of the prettiest villages on the island.

Rates from around 150 to 250 euros per night in season.

**Best for**: families or couples who want sea-facing rooms and easy beach access.

## Premium villa rentals

The genuine luxury experience on the island is often found in private villa rentals rather than hotels. A well-appointed villa with pool, garden and several bedrooms, rented for a week or two, can rival a hotel stay in comfort and surpass it in space and flexibility.

The best villas, particularly those with private pools in central villages, book out very early for July and August. British visitors who typically plan holidays months in advance are well placed to secure the best properties. Booking from October or November for the following summer is realistic for the top tier.

**Price range**: quality villas with pool start from around 250 to 350 euros per night in season (for a property sleeping six to eight). The most prestigious properties reach 800 to 1,000 euros per night in August.

## Price context

These are French island prices in peak season. Converted to sterling at current rates, Hotel de Toiras at 350 euros per night is around 300 pounds. A luxury villa for eight at 600 euros per night works out at around 75 pounds per person. For what you get on the island, these figures compare well with equivalent experiences in the UK.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Hotel de Toiras worth the price?**
For a special occasion, yes. The combination of the Vauban setting, the service and the quality of the rooms makes it genuinely distinctive.

**Do luxury properties on Île de Ré have private pools?**
Hotel de Toiras has an indoor pool. Some villa rentals have private outdoor pools, which are worth the premium in the July and August heat.

**How far in advance should you book?**
For July and August: as early as possible. Hotel de Toiras and Le Richelieu often fill several months in advance for peak summer. Luxury villa rentals with pools can be booked out by November for the following August.

**Is there a spa on the island?**
Yes. Both Hotel de Toiras and La Baronnie have spa facilities. For a day spa visit without staying, La Baronnie sometimes accepts non-residents.
