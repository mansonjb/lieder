Staying close to the beach on the Île de Ré requires care in selection — "beachfront" means different things, and the island's best beaches are often a short cycle from any village.

## What "near the beach" means

On a small island with 100km of cycle paths, every village is near some beach. The question is which beach and how near. For swimming convenience, pertuis-side properties near Sablanceaux, Les Grenettes or the La Couarde south coast are the calmest options.

## Directly on the beach

A small number of properties have direct dune or beach access, mainly on the Atlantic side. These come at a significant premium and require early booking.

## Practical advice

Check the property's exact distance from the beach — count how many minutes on foot, not by car. "5 minutes from the beach" by bike covers 1.5km; by foot it's 400m.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are pertuis or Atlantic properties better for beach access?**
Pertuis for calm family swimming. Atlantic for surf and dramatic scenery. Both are valid depending on your priorities.

**Is it worth paying extra for direct beach access?**
For families who spend most of their time at the water's edge, yes. For those who cycle and explore the island more broadly, the premium may not be worthwhile.
