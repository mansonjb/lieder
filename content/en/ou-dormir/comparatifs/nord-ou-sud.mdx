The north coast and south coast of the Île de Ré offer fundamentally different experiences, and your choice of where to stay shapes your holiday.

## North and Atlantic side

More exposed. Wild beaches. Surf and wind sports. Wilder scenery. Villages like Le Bois-Plage, La Couarde Atlantic side, Ars and Les Portes.

## South and Pertuis side

Calmer. Protected water. Better for families with young children. Warmer sea earlier in the season. Rivedoux, Sainte-Marie, La Flotte.

## The practical answer

Families with young children: south (pertuis) for swimming, north for variety. Active travellers: north (Atlantic) for conditions. Couples: either, depending on aesthetic preference.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you access both coastlines from the same base?**
Yes — the island is narrow enough that cycling between the two sides takes 10–20 minutes from central villages.

**Is the north coast colder?**
The Atlantic side has slightly cooler water and more wind, but temperature differences are small.
