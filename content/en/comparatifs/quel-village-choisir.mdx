Choosing a village to stay in on the Île de Ré shapes your entire experience.

## Quick reference

| Village | Best for |
|---|---|
| Saint-Martin-de-Ré | Most to do, best hotel choice, most restaurants |
| La Flotte | Most beautiful, best port experience, great market |
| Sainte-Marie-de-Ré | Families, kite sports, good services |
| Rivedoux-Plage | Practical first-stop, bridge access |
| Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré | Best Atlantic beaches, good camping |
| La Couarde-sur-mer | Central position, both coastlines |
| Ars-en-Ré | Nature, salt, one of France's most beautiful |
| Loix | Maximum quiet, marshes, authentic |
| Saint-Clément-des-Baleines | Lighthouse proximity, quiet |
| Les Portes-en-Ré | Wild end, remoteness, committed naturists |

## The honest simplification

First visit: Saint-Martin or La Flotte. Return visit seeking something different: Ars or Loix. Families with children: La Couarde or Le Bois-Plage.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you stay in a quiet village and still have access to all the island's highlights?**
Yes — by bike, everything is accessible from anywhere.
