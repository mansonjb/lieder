Île de Ré and Île de Noirmoutier both offer Atlantic island experiences, but at different scales and in different departments.

## Île de Ré

Bridge access. UNESCO-listed architecture. Strong restaurant and village culture. Premium prices. Very well-developed tourism infrastructure.

## Île de Noirmoutier

Also bridge-connected (at low tide via the Gois causeway, or via a dedicated bridge). More remote feel. Famous for the Bonnotte potato. Quieter, less commercial. Slightly cheaper.

## Key difference

Noirmoutier feels less developed and more genuinely local. Ré has more to offer in terms of villages, restaurants and culture.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which island has better beaches?**
Both have excellent Atlantic and calm-water beaches. The scale and character differs but the quality is comparable.

**Is the Gois causeway on Noirmoutier worth experiencing?**
Yes — the submersible road (only driveable at low tide) is a unique experience.
