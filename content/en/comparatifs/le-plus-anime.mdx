The liveliest village on the Île de Ré, by a comfortable margin, is Saint-Martin-de-Ré.

## Why Saint-Martin is the most animated

The island's only town (in any meaningful sense). The most restaurants, bars, shops and evening activity. The port is the island's social hub. In summer, the quay is animated until late.

## After Saint-Martin

La Flotte has a genuine port-side evening culture, less intense than Saint-Martin but pleasant. Sainte-Marie-de-Ré has the most services after the two main ports.

## The quietest villages

Loix and Les Portes-en-Ré at the other end of the spectrum — essentially nothing in terms of evening entertainment or general animation.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there nightlife on Île de Ré?**
Limited. Saint-Martin has bars open until midnight or so in season. Nothing like a resort nightclub scene.

**Is the island family-friendly in terms of evening entertainment?**
The island's evenings are centred on long dinners, sunset walks and port-side promenades rather than organised entertainment.
