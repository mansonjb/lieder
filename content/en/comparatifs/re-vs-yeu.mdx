Île d'Yeu requires a ferry from the mainland and offers a more adventurous, less polished island experience than Île de Ré.

## Île de Ré

Bridge access. Well-developed tourism. Strong restaurant and village culture.

## Île d'Yeu

Ferry from Fromentine (Vendée). Hillier terrain (unusual for an Atlantic island). Less developed. More rugged beaches. More physical.

## Who chooses Île d'Yeu

Those who've been to Ré and Oléron and want something more remote. Those who specifically like the adventure of ferry access and less commercialised islands.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Île d'Yeu harder to visit than Île de Ré?**
More logistical effort: ferry booking, seasonal schedules, no car access. Worth it for those who want the experience.

**Which is more beautiful?**
Different aesthetic entirely — Yeu's rocky terrain versus Ré's flat, luminous, low-profile character.
