Île de Ré and Île d'Oléron are the two main Atlantic islands off the Charente-Maritime coast, frequently compared.

## Île de Ré

More famous. More expensive. Better villages and architecture. Higher-quality restaurant scene. More variety in a smaller space. The prettier island.

## Île d'Oléron

Larger (roughly twice the size). Cheaper. Less crowded in relative terms. Longer beaches. More forest. Less polished. Better value for space.

## Who should choose which

**Île de Ré:** First-time Atlantic island visitors. Foodies. Those who prioritise village character and atmosphere.

**Île d'Oléron:** Families wanting more space and lower prices. Those who find Ré too expensive or crowded.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you visit both in one trip?**
They're 50km apart by road. A day trip from one to the other is possible but long — better to choose one as your base.

**Is Île d'Oléron less beautiful than Île de Ré?**
Different — Oléron has more forest and wilder terrain. Ré has more refined village character.
