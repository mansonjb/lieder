The Île de Ré has two villages on France's official "most beautiful villages" list: La Flotte and Ars-en-Ré. Choosing between them reveals two distinct approaches to island beauty.

## La Flotte

Port village. Hollyhocks on white walls. Cobbled lanes. Harbour with fishing boats. Market twice a week. The most photogenic village in the classic Île de Ré postcard sense.

## Ars-en-Ré

Salt-working village. Black-spired church (a navigation landmark). Surrounded by marshes. Quieter and more nature-adjacent. France's "most beautiful villages" list includes it for its architectural coherence and authentic character.

## Saint-Martin-de-Ré

Not on the official list but has the most imposing architecture. UNESCO fortifications, the port, the citadel. A different kind of beauty.

## Frequently asked questions

**Which is more beautiful: La Flotte or Ars-en-Ré?**
La Flotte for its port and flower-lined streets. Ars for its marshland setting and distinctive church spire. Personal preference; both deserve their listing.

**Is Saint-Martin-de-Ré beautiful?**
Architecturally grand and historically significant. The fortifications are impressive. Less intimate than La Flotte; more urban.
