The Île de Ré is one of several Atlantic islands off the French coast. Here's how it compares to the alternatives, and how its own villages stack up against each other.

## Île de Ré vs other islands

**Vs Île d'Oléron** — Oléron is larger, cheaper and less crowded. Ré has more character per square kilometre, better villages and higher prices.

**Vs Île de Noirmoutier** — Noirmoutier is more remote, less commercialised. Ré has better infrastructure and more variety. Both have flat cycling.

**Vs Île d'Yeu** — Yeu requires a ferry and is more adventurous, with wilder terrain. Ré is easier for families.

## Choosing a village

The most common question is where to base yourself. The answer depends on your priorities:

- **Most to do on foot:** Saint-Martin-de-Ré
- **Best beaches:** Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré or La Couarde
- **Most authentic:** Ars-en-Ré or Loix
- **Best value:** anywhere except Saint-Martin
- **Best for families:** La Couarde, Le Bois-Plage

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Île de Ré better than Île d'Oléron?**
Depends what you want. Ré: more polished, more expensive, more charming villages. Oléron: bigger, cheaper, rawer.

**Which is the most beautiful village on the island?**
La Flotte and Ars-en-Ré both appear on France's official "most beautiful villages" list. Saint-Martin has the most imposing architecture.
