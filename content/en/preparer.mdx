Planning a trip to the Île de Ré is straightforward once you understand the practicalities.

## Getting there

**By car from Paris:** 4.5–5 hours on the A10 motorway to La Rochelle. The bridge toll (around €16–19 in summer) is a single crossing; return is free.

**By train:** TGV from Paris Montparnasse to La Rochelle in 3 hours. Then taxi, bus or hire car to the island.

**By plane:** La Rochelle has a regional airport with seasonal flights from UK and European cities. Nantes or Bordeaux airports are 1.5–2 hours by car.

## Getting around on the island

Bike. That's the answer for most people. Over 100 km of cycle paths, flat terrain, hire shops in every village. Cars create traffic and parking stress in summer; bikes do not.

Bus service (Lilas) runs between villages but is infrequent. A car is useful if you have mobility issues or large amounts of luggage.

## What to bring

Sun protection is non-negotiable. Wind is common even on sunny days — a light windproof layer. Good walking or cycling shoes. Cash for markets and small shops.

## Booking accommodation

Book as early as possible for July and August — January is not too early. Platforms: Airbnb, Vrbo, Booking.com, local agencies. The Stay22 map on each village page aggregates rates across platforms.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is there a supermarket on the island?**
Yes, several. Rivedoux, Saint-Martin and Le Bois-Plage all have supermarkets. Saint-Martin has the island's largest.

**Is the bridge toll expensive?**
Around €16–19 in peak season, dropping to around €8 in winter. Free to exit. Budget accordingly.
