How long you need on the Île de Ré depends on what you want to do. Here's a practical breakdown by duration.

## One day

Arrive via the bridge, head straight to Saint-Martin-de-Ré for the fortifications and a port-side lunch. Afternoon at a beach — Les Grenettes or Sablanceaux for calm water, Les Gollandières for drama. Back before evening traffic. You've seen the headline, not the island.

## A weekend (2 nights)

Day one: Saint-Martin and the eastern coast. Day two: Ars-en-Ré and a loop through the salt marshes to the lighthouse at Saint-Clément. Dinner both nights at La Flotte or Saint-Martin. A proper taster.

## Three to five days

Enough time to slow down. Visit every village by bike, pick your favourite beach, eat oysters more than once, watch the sunset from the lighthouse. Consider a day trip to Fort Boyard or Île d'Aix by boat.

## A week or more

The right amount for a proper Île de Ré summer. You'll find rhythms — your bakery, your beach, your café. You'll understand why people come back every year.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is a day trip from La Rochelle worth it?**
Yes, but go early and manage expectations: you'll see one or two things well, not the whole island.

**Is a week too long?**
For most people: no. For some, the island's limited variety (beaches, cycling, markets, restaurants) becomes familiar after 5–6 days. Know your travel style.
