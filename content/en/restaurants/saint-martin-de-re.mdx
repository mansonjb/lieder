Saint-Martin-de-Ré is the gastronomic capital of the island, and it shows the moment you walk along the Quai de la Poithevinière. Animated terraces, the smell of grilled fish, a glass of Muscadet in hand — all of this in a UNESCO World Heritage setting. The scene spans oyster bars, relaxed bistronomy and a handful of serious fine-dining tables.

## Our picks

**Le Réfectoire** (bistronomy, €€) — Go for the whole grilled fish or pan-fried shellfish. The chalkboard menu changes with the market, and the house burgers are worth a detour. Quirky décor, laid-back atmosphere, and reliably packed: book well in advance, this is one of the most sought-after tables on the island. On the port, 53 Quai de la Poithevinière.

**Le Gabriel** (port brasserie, €€) — Grilled octopus, tartare, catch of the day, and a tapas formula that lets you graze without overspending. Large terrace with a direct view over the marina, excellent value for a weekday lunch. 59 Quai de la Poithevinière.

**La Marine** (fish & seafood, €€) — Seafood sauerkraut, moules-frites, seafood platters: a reliable classic on the port, ideal for settling in on the terrace and watching the sailboats manoeuvre. In season, opt for the platters over hot dishes.

**Le Tout du Cru** (oyster bar, €€) — Twelve varieties of oysters including the island's own Île de Ré oysters, assembled platters to suit your appetite. This is the place to compare growing areas: Ré versus Marennes, versus Brittany. A glass of Muscadet or Pineau des Charentes is the obvious pairing. Accessible from the port, centre-village.

**Madame Sardine** (fish & seafood, €€) — Fish specialities, fresh shellfish and a sunny terrace on the port side. Convivial and relaxed atmosphere, well suited to groups. A solid option for a summer lunch without any fuss.

**La Table d'Olivia** (gastronomic, €€€) — The most refined table in Saint-Martin: island terroir cuisine executed with precision, quality local produce and careful presentation throughout. The interior garden is exceptional for dinner in summer. Book well ahead — in July and August, tables go weeks in advance.

## The market

Les Halles (2 Rue Jean Jaurès) open daily from 08:30 to 13:00 from April through the end of September. Out of season they operate on Thursdays, Fridays, bank holidays and weekends. In July and August a night market also sets up on the port. This is where you buy Île de Ré oysters direct from producers, AOP Ré potatoes, fleur de sel and local artisan goods. Putting together a picnic on the ramparts is often the best option, particularly with children.

## Frequently asked questions

**Should you book for dinner in Saint-Martin in summer?**
Yes. For the best tables, reservations are essential from late June to early September. Le Réfectoire and La Table d'Olivia fill up weeks in advance. For lunch, aim to arrive before 12:30.

**Are there vegetarian options in Saint-Martin?**
The offer is dominated by seafood, but several restaurants include well-considered vegetarian dishes on their menus. Check directly with individual restaurants before booking.

**Which area has the best concentration of restaurants?**
The Quai de la Poithevinière has the majority of addresses along with the best port-view terraces. For a quieter, more gastronomic setting, La Table d'Olivia in the town centre offers a different atmosphere entirely.
