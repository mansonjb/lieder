For a village of its size, La Flotte-en-Ré punches well above its weight at the table — from casual port-side bistrots to a Michelin-starred address lodged in the oldest house on the harbour. In high season, the best tables fill up several days in advance. Plan ahead or you will be looking at a menu through the window.

## The market first

La Flotte's market runs every morning from 8am to 1pm, April through September (daily except Monday the rest of the year), inside the medieval covered hall at the centre of the village. Butcher, fishmonger, cheesemonger, market gardener, baker — the full complement. On Wednesday and Saturday mornings in summer, non-food traders spill out along Cours Félix Faure. And if you are here in July or August, do not miss the night market on that same street, running every evening from 7pm to 11pm.

## Our picks

**L'Ecailler** (gastronomy, fish and seafood, €€€) — Oysters, lobster, and beautifully handled fish fillets prepared with a sustainable bistronomie approach: this is La Flotte's Michelin star, set inside the oldest building on the harbour, dating from 1652. Ask for a table on the quayside terrace — there is no better seat in the village. Open Wednesday to Sunday, lunch 12:15–2pm, dinner 7:30–10pm. 3 Quai de Sénac.

**Chai Nous Comme Chai Vous** (gastronomy, market cuisine, €€€) — Sea bass in a sand crust, duck foie gras lacquered with Ars-en-Ré seaweed, samphire, cockles, house-made bread and ice creams: chef Laurent Favier's creative menu shifts with every visit. Only 18 covers in a converted family chai, with a wine list of over 100 organic references. Listed in the Michelin guide. Book well in advance — this is not a figure of speech. 1 Rue Garde.

**Le Saint Georges** (bistrot, fish and seafood, €€) — Perfectly cooked scallop skewers, seared tuna, fresh fish and shellfish platters. A reliable value address appreciated by regulars, right across from the old harbour. Generous portions and an honest price for the island. Good for a family lunch without stretching the budget.  10 Quai de Sénac.

**Brasserie de la Plage (HR Beach Brasserie)** (brasserie, seafood, €€€) — Scallop risotto, roast lobster, and pink prawns with a direct view of the sea from the terrace. Sunset from here is genuinely spectacular. Live music some evenings in season. Reviews suggest lunch is the more consistent service — worth bearing in mind if you are after a reliable experience. Plage de La Flotte.

**Le Poséidon** (brasserie, traditional cooking, €€) — Daily set menus around €22, home-cooked dishes that follow the catch, a warm welcome from the owners. Rated excellent by its regulars. A well-executed, unpretentious meal that leaves nothing to complain about. Booking advised in summer. Near camping l'Île Blanche.

**L'Endroit du Goinfre** (relaxed bistrot, generous cooking, €€) — Burgers, meat dishes, and house recipes that make the most of fresh local produce. Generous portions, laid-back atmosphere, and no need to book weeks ahead. The locals' choice for a convivial, no-fuss meal.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do I need to reserve in La Flotte during the season?**
Yes, absolutely for dinner at the top tables from June onwards. In July and August, allow at least three to five days for L'Ecailler and Chai Nous Comme Chai Vous. Weekday lunches at the bistrots occasionally have walk-in availability, but do not count on it.

**Are the restaurants open year-round in La Flotte?**
Several close from November to March. Check opening hours by phone or online before making the trip out of season.

**Can you buy and eat oysters straight from the producers in La Flotte?**
Yes — a few oyster huts around the harbour sell direct and let you eat on the spot. Less formal than a restaurant and often cheaper. Bring bread and lemon.
