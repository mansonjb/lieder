The Île de Ré can be expensive, but there are consistent ways to eat well without paying premium prices.

## Best value approaches

**Set lunches (formules)** — all restaurants offer a two or three-course lunch menu at a fixed price, typically 15–22€. Often the same kitchen producing its best work at lower prices.

**Market eating** — a dozen oysters at the market, eaten standing up with bread and white wine, costs under 15€ and is more satisfying than many restaurant meals.

**Bakeries** — the island's boulangeries produce good bread, pastries and sometimes sandwiches. A reliable lunch option.

**Village restaurants off the tourist trail** — restaurants in less-visited villages (Loix, Saint-Clément) charge less than those in Saint-Martin or La Flotte.

## What costs more

Evening à la carte. Port-side tables in high-profile restaurants. Any restaurant specifically marketed to tourists.

## Frequently asked questions

**What's the average lunch cost for two on a budget?**
30–40€ for two with a carafe of water at a set-menu lunch. Under 20€ at a crêperie or with market food.

**Are there options for picnics?**
Excellent. The markets provide everything you need.
