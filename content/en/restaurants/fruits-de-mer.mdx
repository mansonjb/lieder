Seafood is the island's primary gastronomic language — the combination of Atlantic fishing, local oyster production and shellfish gathering produces outstanding raw materials.

## What's available

Oysters (cupped and flat), mussels, clams, cockles, periwinkles, whelks, langoustines (in season), crab, sea bass, sole, dorade and whatever the boats brought in that morning.

## Best places

Port-side restaurants in La Flotte and Saint-Martin have the best seafood presentation. Market stalls offer the most direct experience.

## Shellfish platters

A seafood platter (plateau de fruits de mer) is the classic order for groups — oysters, mussels, prawns, crab on ice. Order for a minimum of two people.

## Frequently asked questions

**When is the best season for seafood on Île de Ré?**
Oysters year-round. Langoustines in spring and early summer. Mussels in summer. The freshest fish varies with what the boats bring in.

**Are there shellfish allergy considerations?**
Obviously — confirm ingredients if you have allergies. Most restaurants are used to the question.
