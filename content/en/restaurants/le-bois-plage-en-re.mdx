Le Bois-Plage-en-Ré eats well without any fuss. It is not Saint-Martin with its harbour-front terraces, but it has its own solid addresses rooted in the island's produce: full-sea oysters, the day's catch, and that easy terrace atmosphere under the pines that makes a meal here feel genuinely Rétais. In high season some tables fill fast — booking the day before is often a good idea.

## Our picks

**Les Q Salés** (Bistrot & oyster bar, €€) — The market square, a chilled bottle of white wine, and a plate of oysters from the island. This is the go-to spot for a terrace apéro, drawing locals and summer visitors in equal measure. The daily specials on the chalkboard change every week. Open year-round.

**Le Moulin à Café** (Brasserie & wine bar, €€) — Oysters, moules-frites, duck confit or homemade pizza depending on your mood: the menu is honest and the produce is fresh. Worth knowing: they do not take reservations. Arrive early to avoid a wait, especially at weekends. Closed on Tuesdays out of season.

**Les Boisilles** (Fish & seafood, €€) — Chef Christophe works with wild fish from the local catch, on a short menu that changes weekly according to what comes in. Check the daily specials on the terrace board before you sit down — that is usually where the best finds are hiding.

**Hôtel Restaurant l'Océan** (Fish & seafood, €€€) — Fish soup, fresh fillets of the day, pan-fried prawns. Careful, sea-focused cooking rated by Gault & Millau (11/20) and ranked the village's top restaurant online with over 1,000 reviews. Weekday lunch dish of the day at €26. Booking recommended in season.

**Restaurant Plaisir** (Gastronomic, €€€) — Refined cooking from local Île de Ré produce, with the Maître Restaurateur label and dinner served on a poolside terrace under the pines. For a proper special occasion. Dinner only, 7 pm to 9 pm, closed at lunchtime and on Sunday evenings. Reservations essential.

**Eau P'Tit Bois** (Crêperie & brasserie, €) — Galettes, mussels and homemade ice cream a few steps from the Gollandières beach. Open every day from 8 am to 2 am, no reservation needed. The ideal option for a light lunch after a surf session or a late-afternoon snack with the family.

## The Bois-Plage market

The island's largest market takes place on the village square, right in the centre of Le Bois-Plage. In season (1 April to 1 November) it runs every day from 8:30 am until 1 pm. Out of season: Tuesdays, Thursdays, Saturdays and Sundays. Local vegetables, fish, island oysters, bread, honey and fleur de sel — everything you need to put together a quality meal without setting foot in a restaurant. The terrace of Les Q Salés sits right beside it, at the heart of village life.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are there restaurants open in Le Bois-Plage out of season?**
Yes, a few addresses stay open year-round, including Les Q Salés and Hôtel Restaurant l'Océan. The choice narrows in winter, but the village is far from dead. Check opening hours directly with each place before making the trip.

**Do I need to book in Le Bois-Plage in July and August?**
For Hôtel Restaurant l'Océan and Restaurant Plaisir, yes — without hesitation. For Le Moulin à Café, reservations are not accepted: just arrive early. The other addresses generally take bookings and it is advisable at weekends.

**Are there restaurants on the beach at Le Bois-Plage?**
Not directly on the sand, but Eau P'Tit Bois and Les Boisilles are right by the beach, just a few metres from Les Gollandières. In season, a handful of seasonal snack stands fill in the gaps near the main beach access points.
