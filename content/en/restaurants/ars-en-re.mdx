Ars-en-Ré does food well and unpretentiously. The harbour, the black-and-white bell tower, the salt marshes as a backdrop — and on the plate, fish landed that morning and oysters harvested a few kilometres away. Less polished than Saint-Martin, but the quality of produce is beyond dispute. In July and August tourist pressure is real, and booking becomes virtually essential everywhere.

## Our picks

**La Cabane du Fier** (Seafood, ££) — The standout address on the edge of the village, looking out over the Fier d'Ars. Wood-fired grilled fish, seafood platters, island oysters and a Charentaise chowder that is worth the trip alone. The menu follows what comes in off the boats, so it changes — a good sign. Arrive early in season to secure a table with a view over the Fier.

**Chez Rémi** (Seafood, ££) — Right on the harbour at 1 Quai de la Crée. Impeccably fresh, local produce cooked simply and without fuss. The waterside terrace is one of the most pleasant spots in the village — over 580 reviews on Tripadvisor at 4.2/5 says everything. Book ahead without fail in high season.

**La Tour du Sénéchal** (Wine bar and bistrot, ££) — On Place Carnot, facing the famous bell tower. The address picked out by Le Fooding: a blackboard menu of market dishes, a wine bar and an épicerie fine. Everything is made in-house. Works equally well for an afternoon glass or a full meal — the kind of place you wish you had found earlier (4.5/5).

**Le Café du Commerce** (Brasserie, ££) — A harbour institution at 6 Quai de la Prée, open seven days a week from 9.30am to 11pm. Seafood, buckwheat galettes, burgers, grills — a deliberately eclectic menu that makes no apologies. The glazed pergola facing the harbour is perfect in overcast weather, and the decor — saloon mirrors, vintage American glassware — is worth a look in itself. Wheelchair accessible.

**Juline** (Seasonal and gastronomic, £££) — A discreet address in the village centre focused on local produce and short supply chains: cooking that sits between the ocean and the salt marshes, carefully done and genuinely seasonal. A small, personal restaurant — book ahead.

## The market and oysters direct from the producer

Ars has two complementary markets. From early April to the end of September, the harbour market runs every day from 8am to 1.30pm directly on the quayside: local producers, fruit and vegetables, oysters sold direct, and craftspeople. Out of season (October to March) it moves to Place Carnot on Tuesday, Friday and Saturday mornings. In July and August, a evening makers' market takes place on the first and third Mondays of the month from 6pm to 11pm.

For oysters straight from the producer, several cabins are reachable on foot or by bike from the village. Bring bread and butter — some producers will lend you an oyster knife. Sitting facing the marshes, it is one of the best food experiences on the island for under €12 a dozen.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is Ars-en-Ré better than Saint-Martin for eating out?**
They are different. Ars is quieter and more genuine; Saint-Martin has more choice and some high-end gastronomic tables. For a relaxed, local experience Ars is often the better call. For a special evening out, Saint-Martin has the edge.

**Do you need to book in Ars in season?**
Yes — in July and August booking is virtually essential everywhere, especially for dinner. Lead times are shorter than in Saint-Martin though: the day before or two days ahead is usually enough.

**Are there restaurants with a view over the marshes or the harbour?**
Yes. La Cabane du Fier has a fine view over the Fier d'Ars, while Chez Rémi and Le Café du Commerce both have terraces directly on the harbour.
