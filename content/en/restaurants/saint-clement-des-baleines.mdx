At the foot of the Phare des Baleines, Saint-Clément-des-Baleines punches well above its size when it comes to eating out. The village at Île de Ré's western tip offers a genuine dining scene, from relaxed maritime brasseries clustered around the lighthouse to a gastronomic table that draws food lovers from across the island. The one caveat: outside July and August several spots close or cut their hours, so always check before making the trip in low season.

## Our picks

**La Cabane** (Seafood, €€) — The place to try a traditional eclade de moules, the regional ritual of mussels roasted on a pine-board fire. It only runs from May to October, but the house fish parillade is a worthy alternative whenever you visit. Book ahead in high season. A children's menu at €12 makes it an easy family choice. Located on the Allée du Phare, a short walk from the lighthouse.

**JUSTE Restaurant** (Seafood, €€) — A solid reputation backed by over 1,000 reviews averaging 4.4/5. The plateaux de fruits de mer, lobster and chips, and local oysters are the draws here, enjoyed on a lovely terrace facing the lighthouse. In July and August, a reservation is not optional — it's essential. 61 allée du Phare.

**Le Café du Phare** (Brasserie / Seafood, €€) — The most flexible option in the village, open seven days a week from 10am to 10pm with brasserie service at both lunch and dinner. Expect fresh catch of the day, seafood platters, and local craft beers. It also works perfectly as a late-afternoon drinks stop with a lighthouse view from the terrace. 99 Chemin du Passage du Nord, beachside.

**Le Chat Botté** (Gastronomic, €€€) — The restaurant attached to the boutique hotel of the same name, in the village centre. The cooking is precise and seasonal: sea bass in a pastry crust with beurre blanc, scallops with truffle risotto, sweetbreads. The set menu at €26.50 is widely cited as one of the best value-for-money meals at this level on the whole island. Booking is essential. Rue de la Mairie.

**A l'Ouest** (Crêperie / Creative French, €) — Don't be put off by the crêperie label: the kitchen turns out crab and grapefruit salad, cuttlefish fricassee with early-season vegetables, and fresh fish, with a Gault & Millau mention to its name. Menus run from €16 to €20. Private parking and dogs welcome. 6 Mail du Clocher, village centre.

## The village market

The square by the church (place du Clocher) hosts a local producers' market. In the shoulder season (February to June) it runs on Tuesdays, Thursdays and Saturdays from 8am to 1pm. From July to 14 September it goes daily, seven days a week including public holidays. Expect local oysters, Ré new potatoes, fleur de sel, marsh vegetables, and cheeses — ideal for stocking up before a beach day or a picnic in the Trousse-Chemise forest.

## Frequently asked questions

**Can you eat well right next to the lighthouse?**
Yes. JUSTE Restaurant and Le Café du Phare are both on the Allée du Phare, a few minutes' walk from the lighthouse itself. In low season, check opening hours as several places reduce their service from November to April.

**Which restaurant is best for a special dinner in Saint-Clément?**
Le Chat Botté is the unanimous choice for a proper evening meal. Book well ahead, especially in summer.

**Are there good options on a tight budget?**
A l'Ouest offers menus between €16 and €20 with cooking that genuinely outperforms the price point. The village market is also a great starting point for a picnic in the nearby Trousse-Chemise forest.

**Are restaurants open outside the summer season?**
Several close from November to April, including La Cabane. Le Café du Phare stays open year-round seven days a week, but always confirm before making a special trip in low season.
