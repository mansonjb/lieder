The oyster shacks (cabanes ostréicoles) of the Île de Ré offer the most direct oyster experience on the island — eaten fresh from the producer at the water's edge.

## What they are

Small wooden shacks operated by the oyster farmers themselves, where the shellfish are opened and served with bread, butter and often a glass of wine or local white.

## Where to find them

La Flotte has some of the best-known shacks. The area around Sainte-Marie and La Couarde also has producers who sell direct. Ask locally for current-season recommendations.

## The experience

No table service, no menu in the formal sense. You buy by the half-dozen or dozen, the producer opens them in front of you, and you eat them at the simplest possible table with the simplest possible accompaniments.

## When they're open

Primarily in season (May to September). Hours vary. Some are open year-round.

## Frequently asked questions

**Is eating oysters at the cabane more expensive than at a restaurant?**
Cheaper. You're buying closer to the source.

**What wine should you drink with Île de Ré oysters?**
A dry white: Muscadet, Entre-Deux-Mers, or a dry Charentes white. Avoid tannic reds.
