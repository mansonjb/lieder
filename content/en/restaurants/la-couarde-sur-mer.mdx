La Couarde-sur-Mer is one of those rare island villages where the dining scene genuinely rewards exploration. Between oyster shacks tucked into the marshes, relaxed village bistros, and a gastronomic institution that has been going since 1936, there is plenty to keep you eating well without ever leaving the commune. In high season, book ahead — the best tables fill up by midweek for the weekend.

## Our picks

**Le Balaou Île de Ré** (Creative bistro / French with Creole influences, €€) — The standout surprise in La Couarde. House-made cod fritters, oysters grilled with Thai basil, a French kitchen that borrows Caribbean notes without ever feeling forced. Expect around 25 euros for a full meal. One of the most sought-after tables on the island, so book well in advance in season. Located close to the Peu Ragot beach.

**La Pergola** (Gastronomic / Refined French cuisine, €€€) — An institution since 1936. Chef Fabien Devaine works with local produce from across the island: tapas, ceviche, carefully sourced Basque charcuterie. The atmosphere is polished without being stiff. Budget 30-40 euros per person and reserve your table. Situated in the village centre.

**La Cabane du Feneau** (Oyster shack / Seafood, €€) — Fresh Feneau oysters, seafood platters, grilled mussels and plancha cooking in the evenings. This is Île de Ré eating at its most authentic: cycle out along the marsh bike paths, arrive at 18h30 when it opens in July and August, and eat with your feet in the salt air. Book the best tables if you can. Found at the Platière de la Passe, between La Couarde and Loix.

**La Cale** (Bistro / Market cuisine, €€) — The best value-for-money address in the village, rated 4.4 out of 5 across more than 530 reviews. The éclade de moules — mussels cooked over pine needles — is worth seeking out, as are the langoustines on the plancha. Reasonable prices by island standards. On avenue du Mail.

**Bistrot Chez Lulu** (Bistro / Home cooking, €€) — Everything is made in-house, the dishes are designed for sharing, and the fish and vegetables come straight from local producers. A relaxed, convivial atmosphere that suits families and groups equally well. No fuss, just good food.

**Les Mouettes** (French / Seasonal bistro, €€) — Seasonal home-cooked dishes, a practical lunch formula, and a handy takeaway option if you want to eat on the beach without the faff. Pleasant terrace and patio in the village centre.

## The market: the right way to start the day

La Couarde market is one of the most family-friendly on the island. In peak season (mid-June to mid-September) it runs daily. Out of season it takes place on Wednesday, Thursday, Saturday and Sunday. Look for Raphaël under the covered hall — he sells his own oysters and you can taste them on the spot. Alongside seasonal fruit and vegetables, fresh fish and local crafts, it is the perfect place to assemble a beach picnic or take home a box of oysters.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do you need to book ahead in La Couarde during summer?**
For Le Balaou and La Pergola, absolutely — several days in advance in July and August. For the other addresses, the day before usually works, but calling ahead is always worth it.

**Where can I buy oysters directly from the producer?**
La Cabane du Feneau in the marshes between La Couarde and Loix, or Raphaël at the village covered market. Both let you taste on site.

**Can you eat with a sea view?**
Not right on the beach, but the takeaway option at Les Mouettes is made for exactly that. La Cabane du Feneau, reached by bike along the marsh paths, offers a wild, open setting you will not find anywhere else.

**Are restaurants open all year?**
Several establishments close between November and March. In low season, always check opening hours before making the trip. La Pergola tends to stay open longer than most.
