The first village you reach after crossing the bridge, Sainte-Marie-de-Ré sets the tone immediately: eating local is not a trend here, it is simply how things work. From the highly regarded market bistronomy in the village centre to the gastronomic restaurant with sea views at the Relais Thalasso, the range is genuinely complete — family lunches under 15 euros and special-occasion dinners sit comfortably side by side.

## Our picks

**Le Chai** (Market bistronomy, €€) — The menu changes every morning based on what arrives from the fish market: wild-caught fish, island oysters, organic vegetables from local market gardeners. Rated 4.8/5, it is the most sought-after address in Sainte-Marie. Sitting right on the Place d'Antioche next to the market, the natural programme is to browse the stalls in the morning then settle in for lunch. Book ahead — arriving early gives you the widest choice as the kitchen works strictly with what is available that day.

**L'Atalante** (Gastronomic, €€€) — The restaurant of the Relais Thalasso, open to everyone, not just hotel guests. Natural oysters from Rivedoux-Plage, Ré seaweed, PDO potatoes, Sainte-Marie saffron: a sustainable kitchen drawing on 99% French produce. Ask for a terrace table facing the sea. Booking is essential in season.

**Le Brasero** (Traditional French, €€) — Moules-frites, honest terroir cooking, fresh seasonal ingredients. Set within the Les Grenettes estate, fully renovated in 2021, it is the reliable choice at the island's entrance for a meal without fuss. Average spend around 35 euros. Reservations available through TheFork.

**Restaurant Oyat** (French cuisine / Crêperie, €€) — An intimate address on the village square in La Noue, with a shaded terrace under the lime trees or a cosy dining room inside. Sincere seasonal cooking, crêpes and galettes, relaxed atmosphere and a warm welcome. The right option when you want to eat well without any ceremony.

**Les Tilleuls** (Crêperie / French cuisine, €) — A fixture on the Place des Tilleuls in La Noue for over 15 years. The daily set menu at 14.90 euros, served with a café gourmand, is ideal for families at lunch. Shaded terrace beneath the lime trees. The best value for money in the area.

## Sainte-Marie market

The market is held on the Place d'Antioche, the main square of the village. Since 2021 a covered market hall complements the outdoor stalls. Fresh vegetables, oysters, honey, farm produce: the best of what the island grows and harvests gathers here.

Hours: from 1 April to 30 June, Tuesday, Wednesday, Friday, Saturday and Sunday, 8:30 to 13:00. From 1 July to 15 September, every day. Combining a morning at the market with lunch at Le Chai right next door is the perfect half-day plan in Sainte-Marie.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are restaurants in Sainte-Marie-de-Ré open outside the summer season?**
Some close between October and March. Les Tilleuls and a few brasseries run year-round. L'Atalante at the Relais Thalasso operates continuously. Check opening hours before visiting in the low season.

**Do I need to book in Sainte-Marie in summer?**
For Le Chai and L'Atalante, booking is essential — often several days in advance during July and August. For other addresses, an evening reservation is strongly recommended. Lunch is more flexible, but terraces fill up quickly between noon and 2 pm.

**Where can families eat on a reasonable budget?**
Les Tilleuls in La Noue with its 14.90-euro set menu and Restaurant Oyat are the best options. Le Brasero at Les Grenettes also suits families well, with an average bill around 35 euros for two.
