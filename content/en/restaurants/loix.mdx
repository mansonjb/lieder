Eating in Loix means embracing the village's character: a small, selective scene built entirely around local produce — oysters from the Pointe du Grouin, fleur de sel from the marshes, island-grown vegetables. In high season, terrace tables fill up by noon. Book ahead or arrive early.

## The market first

The Place du Marché is the true heart of Loix. In summer, the outdoor and covered market runs daily from 8am to 1pm (until 2.30pm during French school holidays) — local producers, oysters, island vegetables, artisans. Off season, it shrinks to Tuesdays and Fridays. Start here before choosing where to eat.

## Our picks

**La Cabane du Grouin** (Seafood, ££) — Perched right at the Pointe du Grouin with views over the marshes, this cabin serves oysters and king prawns from their own aquaculture operation. The tartare is clean and precise, the langoustines need no introduction. If pan-fried oysters with yuzu and cream are on the board, order them. Open Easter to All Saints' Day; book ahead in July and August.

**La Route du Sel** (Bistrot / French, ££) — Running since 1999 at 9 Place de l'Eglise, this place cooks with fresh herbs from its own garden and it shows. Go for the Bouchot mussels, buckwheat galettes, or grilled squid with aromatics. The salted-butter caramel dessert made with fleur de sel is non-negotiable. Lunch menu around €16-20, with a pleasant terrace overlooking the church.

**La Presqu'ile** (Brasserie, ££) — A terrace directly on the market square, ideal after a walk through the salt marshes. The menu follows the catch of the day, with generous burgers and fresh salads alongside. Tables can be tight in high season — a lunch visit tends to be more relaxed than an evening one.

**La Cabane du Feneau** (Oyster cabin, £) — Reachable by bike from Loix, tucked in the marshes between Loix and La Couarde-sur-Mer. Oysters, prawns, and rillettes through the day. In July and August, the plancha fires up from 6.30pm — and the sunset over the marshes comes at no extra charge. One of the most genuinely authentic spots on the island.

**Les Ilates** (Pizzeria, £) — Route du Grouin, Le Petit Boucheau. The affordable, family-friendly option away from the village centre, easy to reach by bike. Pizzas, salads, burgers. Useful when every other terrace is full.

## Frequently asked questions

**Are restaurants open in Loix in winter?**
Barely. Outside spring and autumn weekends, there is almost no dining open in Loix. Always check before planning a meal there, and pack your own food if you are staying off season.

**Should I book a table in Loix in summer?**
Yes, without hesitation for La Cabane du Grouin and La Route du Sel. Small terraces fill fast. For the oyster cabins, queuing is part of the experience, but arriving before the rush saves disappointment.

**Can I buy fleur de sel directly in Loix?**
Yes. Some salt workers sell directly from their marshes. Ask at the Écomusée du marais salant or at the market — local producers often have a stall.

**Is Loix manageable without a car?**
Entirely, as long as you plan. A bike covers everything: the market in the morning, an oyster cabin in the afternoon, a terrace in the evening. La Cabane du Feneau and Les Ilates are both reachable by bike from the village. For a livelier evening, Ars-en-Ré is about 15 minutes' pedalling away.
