The Île de Ré is a thin sliver of Atlantic island connected to La Rochelle by a 3-kilometre toll bridge. Just over 30 kilometres long and rarely more than 5 wide, it packs in salt marshes, oyster beds, pine forests, whitewashed villages and some of the finest beaches on France's western coast.

## What makes the island special

Ré runs at its own pace. Bicycles outnumber cars in the villages. The light is luminous — Atlantic light, flat and bright, the kind that photographers and painters have been chasing here for decades. Roses trémières (hollyhocks) climb every stone wall from June to August, and the smell of salt is constant.

The island has ten distinct communes, each with its own personality. Saint-Martin-de-Ré is the fortified main town, a UNESCO World Heritage site. Ars-en-Ré is quieter, famous for its salt marshes and black-spired church. Les Portes-en-Ré at the western tip is the wildest. Rivedoux-Plage, first village after the bridge, is the most accessible.

## Beaches

The island has two coastlines. The Atlantic side faces west: open beaches, waves, surf, wild dunes. The Pertuis side faces east toward the mainland: calmer water, shallower, better for families with small children. Both are sandy, both beautiful.

## Getting there

From La Rochelle by car: 20–30 minutes to Rivedoux. By TGV to La Rochelle then a 20-minute taxi or bus. No ferry needed — the bridge handles it all.

## When to visit

July and August are the busiest months, with the island's 18,000 permanent residents swelling to over 100,000 visitors. June and September are ideal: warm enough to swim, uncrowded enough to enjoy. October through April is quiet, wild and genuinely local.

## Frequently asked questions

**Do you need a car on Île de Ré?**
No. The island has over 100 km of cycle paths connecting every village. Most visitors hire bikes and leave the car at the campsite or rental house.

**Is Île de Ré expensive?**
More than the French mainland average, but comparable to other popular Atlantic islands. Accommodation and restaurants are pricier in peak season — plan ahead and book early.
